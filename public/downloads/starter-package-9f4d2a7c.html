1. Garlic Butter Chicken Skillet

  • 490 cal
  • Prep: 5 min
  • Cook: 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (6–7 oz each), pounded to ½-inch thickness
  • 3 tbsp butter, divided
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions

  1. Pat chicken dry with paper towels. Season both sides with paprika, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Place chicken in skillet and sear 4–5 minutes per side until golden and cooked through (internal temp 165°F). Remove to a plate.
  4. Reduce heat to medium. Add 3 tbsp butter and minced garlic; cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Stir in honey. Return chicken to skillet and spoon sauce over top. Cook 1 minute.
  6. Transfer to serving plate and garnish with fresh parsley.
Protein: 44 g
Carbs: 6 g
Fat: 22 g
Fiber: 0 g

Meal Prep Notes

Cook chicken and store in airtight container for 4 days. Reheat gently in microwave or skillet. Sauce is best made fresh but can be prepped the night before.

2. Lemon Herb Salmon Plate

  • 520 cal
  • Prep: 5 min
  • Cook: 12 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (5–6 oz each)
  • 4 cups mixed greens (arugula, spinach, or mesclun)
  • 1 cup roasted asparagus (12 spears), trimmed
  • 1 lemon (half juiced for dressing, half for wedges)
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 1 tsp fresh dill (or ½ tsp dried)
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika

Instructions

  1. Pat salmon dry. Season with salt, pepper, dill, and paprika on both sides.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Place salmon skin-side down and sear 4–5 minutes until skin is crispy.
  3. Flip and cook 2–3 minutes until flesh flakes easily with a fork (internal temp 145°F).
  4. Toss greens with 1 tbsp olive oil, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt.
  5. Plate greens, top with salmon, add roasted asparagus, and serve with lemon wedge.
Protein: 38 g
Carbs: 8 g
Fat: 28 g
Fiber: 2 g

Meal Prep Notes

Roast asparagus in bulk on Sunday (toss 1 lb with 1 tbsp oil, salt, pepper; roast at 400°F for 15 min). Store separately for 5 days. Salmon best eaten fresh.

3. Turkey Taco Lettuce Cups

  • 380 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 12 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lean ground turkey (93/7)
  • 2 tbsp gluten-free or regular taco seasoning
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 large head romaine lettuce (8–10 leaves per serving)
  • 1 cup diced tomato (1 large tomato)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • ½ cup salsa (optional)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add ground turkey, breaking it into small pieces as it cooks. Cook 6–7 minutes until browned and no pink remains.
  3. Sprinkle taco seasoning over turkey, add water, and stir well. Simmer 2–3 minutes until liquid thickens.
  4. Carefully separate and stack lettuce leaves on a plate.
  5. Spoon seasoned turkey into each lettuce cup. Top with tomato, avocado, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.
Protein: 36 g
Carbs: 12 g
Fat: 18 g
Fiber: 4 g

Meal Prep Notes

Cook turkey mixture and store in container for 4 days. Assemble lettuce cups fresh to prevent sogginess. Slice avocado just before serving to prevent browning.

5. Chickpea Power Salad

  • 420 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 0 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, rinsed and drained (about 1.5 cups)
  • 1 English cucumber, diced (about 2 cups)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced (about 1 cup)
  • ½ red onion, finely diced (about ½ cup)
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to make dressing.
  2. In a large bowl, combine chickpeas, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, feta, and parsley.
  3. Pour dressing over salad and gently toss until everything is coated.
  4. Chill in refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving (flavors improve as it sits).
Protein: 16 g
Carbs: 34 g
Fat: 16 g
Fiber: 8 g

Meal Prep Notes

Stores beautifully for 4 days in the fridge. Make a big batch on Sunday. Can add grilled chicken (6 oz per serving) for extra protein at dinner.

6. Chicken Fajita Cauliflower Rice Bowl

  • 480 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 18 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (6–7 oz each), sliced into thin strips
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into strips (about 1 cup)
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced into strips (about 1 cup)
  • 1 yellow onion, sliced into strips (about 1 cup)
  • 3 cups cauliflower rice (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tbsp gluten-free or regular fajita seasoning
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil or olive oil, divided
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • ½ tsp sea salt (plus more to taste)

Instructions

  1. Toss chicken strips with 1 tbsp fajita seasoning and 1 tbsp oil in a bowl.
  2. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Cook chicken 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until cooked through. Remove to a plate.
  3. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to skillet. Sauté peppers and onion 4–5 minutes until tender-crisp.
  4. Return chicken to skillet, add remaining fajita seasoning, and toss to combine. Cook 1 minute.
  5. In a separate microwave-safe bowl or skillet, warm cauliflower rice 2–3 minutes until heated through.
  6. Divide cauliflower rice between two bowls, top with fajita mixture, squeeze lime over, and garnish with cilantro.
Protein: 38 g
Carbs: 12 g
Fat: 14 g
Fiber: 3 g

Meal Prep Notes

Slice peppers and onions Sunday for quick weeknight prep. Store separately from chicken. Assemble fresh or reheat gently in microwave.

8. Honey Garlic Shrimp Sheet Pan

  • 420 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 12 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb large shrimp (about 16–20 count), peeled and deveined
  • 1 large head broccoli, cut into florets (about 4 cups)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp tamari or low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp raw honey
  • 1 tbsp avocado or sesame oil
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds (optional garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together tamari, honey, garlic, ginger, oil, salt, and red pepper flakes.
  3. Arrange shrimp and broccoli florets on the sheet pan, spacing them evenly.
  4. Pour about two-thirds of the sauce over the shrimp and broccoli; toss gently to coat.
  5. Roast for 8–10 minutes until shrimp are pink and opaque, and broccoli is tender.
  6. Drizzle remaining sauce over top, sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired, and serve immediately.
Protein: 32 g
Carbs: 18 g
Fat: 8 g
Fiber: 3 g

Meal Prep Notes

Prepare sauce and cut broccoli the night before. Peeled shrimp store for 2 days raw. Best eaten fresh from the oven, but can reheat gently.

9. Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

  • 510 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 12 oz flank or sirloin steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 1 large head broccoli, cut into small florets (about 4 cups)
  • 3 tbsp tamari or low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp raw honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tsp cornstarch (or arrowroot powder)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp avocado or peanut oil, divided
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup cooked brown rice or white rice per serving (½ cup cooked)

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together tamari, vinegar, honey, cornstarch, garlic, and ginger to make sauce.
  2. Pat beef dry and season lightly with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat until shimmering.
  4. Working in batches if needed, cook beef 2 minutes per side until just browned. Remove to a plate.
  5. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the wok. Add broccoli florets and ¼ cup water; stir-fry 3–4 minutes until tender-crisp.
  6. Return beef to wok, pour sauce over everything, and toss constantly for 1–2 minutes until sauce thickens and coats beef and broccoli.
  7. Serve over cooked rice.
Protein: 36 g
Carbs: 32 g
Fat: 14 g
Fiber: 3 g

Meal Prep Notes

Slice beef and chop broccoli ahead of time. Store separately in containers. Cook rice in bulk on Sunday. Stir-fry is best made fresh but can be reheated gently.

10. Cajun Cod with Green Beans

  • 360 cal
  • Prep: 5 min
  • Cook: 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 cod fillets (5–6 oz each)
  • 12 oz fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 1 tbsp gluten-free Cajun seasoning (check label)
  • 2 tbsp avocado or olive oil, divided
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges

Instructions

  1. Pat cod fillets dry with paper towels. Season both sides evenly with Cajun seasoning.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Place seasoned cod in the skillet and sear 3–4 minutes per side until flaky and opaque (internal temp 145°F). Remove to a plate.
  4. In a separate pan, heat remaining 1 tbsp oil over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add green beans, salt, and pepper. Sauté 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until beans are tender-crisp.
  6. Plate cod with green beans on the side. Serve with lemon wedges.
Protein: 34 g
Carbs: 10 g
Fat: 10 g
Fiber: 2 g

Meal Prep Notes

Trim green beans on Sunday and store in containers. Cod is best cooked fresh but can be stored 2 days raw. Tilapia or haddock work as 1:1 substitutes.

11. Buffalo Chicken Stuffed Peppers

  • 480 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 25 min
  • Serves 2 (4 peppers total)

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (red, yellow, or orange), tops cut off and seeds removed
  • 1 lb cooked shredded chicken (about 3 cups) or rotisserie chicken
  • ⅓ cup gluten-free buffalo sauce (Frank's RedHot original is GF)
  • ½ cup cooked brown rice or cauliflower rice
  • ½ cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp plain Greek yogurt or ranch dressing (for drizzling)
  • 2 tbsp sliced green onion, for garnish
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Lightly oil a 9x13 baking dish and place peppers upright inside.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine shredded chicken, buffalo sauce, rice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Divide filling evenly among the 4 peppers, mounding slightly.
  4. Top each pepper with 2 tbsp shredded cheddar cheese.
  5. Bake for 22–25 minutes until peppers are softened and cheese is melted.
  6. Remove from oven. Drizzle lightly with Greek yogurt or ranch, garnish with green onion, and serve hot.
Protein: 40 g
Carbs: 18 g
Fat: 12 g
Fiber: 3 g

Meal Prep Notes

Stuff peppers the night before and bake fresh for dinner. Or make ahead and reheat at 350°F for 12–15 min. Store for up to 3 days.

12. Pesto Zoodle Turkey Pasta

  • 420 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 12 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey (93/7), or turkey breast, finely diced
  • 4 medium zucchini, spiralized into noodles
  • ⅓ cup fresh or store-bought pesto (verify gluten-free)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ⅛ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add ground turkey, breaking it into small pieces as it cooks. Cook 6–7 minutes until browned and no pink remains. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Add cherry tomato halves to the pan and cook 2 minutes until they just begin to soften and release their juices.
  4. Add spiralized zucchini noodles and pesto to the skillet. Toss gently for 2–3 minutes until zoodles are just tender (do not overcook or they will become mushy).
  5. Remove from heat. Top with Parmesan, red pepper flakes if desired, and fresh basil.
  6. Divide between two plates and serve immediately.
Protein: 36 g
Carbs: 12 g
Fat: 16 g
Fiber: 3 g

Meal Prep Notes

Spiralize zucchini up to 1 day ahead; store in a colander lined with paper towels to drain excess liquid. Cook turkey component ahead and reheat gently. Assemble fresh with zoodles to prevent sogginess.

13. Mediterranean Chicken Power Bowl

  • 540 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 25 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (6–7 oz each), grilled or pan-seared and sliced
  • 1 cup cooked brown rice
  • 1 cup diced cucumber
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

Instructions

  1. Season chicken breasts with ½ tsp salt, ¼ tsp pepper, and ½ tsp oregano on both sides.

14. Clean Breakfast Scramble

  • 380 cal
  • Prep: 5 min
  • Cook: 10 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ lb lean turkey sausage (ground or links, crumbled)
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced (about ¾ cup)
  • ¼ cup diced onion
  • 1 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ tsp garlic powder
  • Fresh chives or parsley, chopped (optional garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Set aside.
  2. Heat ½ tbsp butter in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add crumbled turkey sausage and cook 4–5 minutes, breaking it apart as it cooks, until browned and no pink remains. Remove to a plate.
  4. Add remaining ½ tbsp butter to the same skillet. Add diced bell pepper and onion; sauté 2–3 minutes until softened.
  5. Add spinach and cook 1 minute until just wilted.
  6. Return sausage to the skillet. Pour whisked eggs over everything and gently scramble, stirring occasionally, for 2–3 minutes until eggs are cooked through but still soft.
  7. Transfer to serving plates, garnish with fresh chives if desired, and serve hot.
Protein: 32 g
Carbs: 8 g
Fat: 18 g
Fiber: 1 g

Meal Prep Notes

Dice vegetables Sunday evening. Cook turkey sausage in bulk for grab-and-go breakfasts. Scramble is best fresh but can be reheated gently in the microwave (30–40 sec).

15. Moroccan Beef Tagine

A rich, slow-simmered Moroccan classic featuring tender beef, aromatic spices, vegetables, and naturally sweet dried apricots. Perfect for family dinners or meal prep.

  • 428 cal
  • Prep: 20 min
  • Cook: 2 hr
  • Serves 6

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck roast, cut into 1½-inch cubes
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced into half moons
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup low-sodium beef broth
  • ½ cup dried apricots, chopped
  • ¼ cup green olives
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro
  • Juice of ½ lemon

Instructions

  1. Pat the beef dry with paper towels and season lightly with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Brown the beef in batches for 6–8 minutes until all sides are caramelized. Remove and set aside.
  4. Reduce heat to medium and sauté the onion for 5 minutes until translucent.
  5. Add the minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
  6. Stir in the cumin, paprika, cinnamon, ginger, and turmeric. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  7. Add diced tomatoes, beef broth, carrots, dried apricots, and return the beef to the pot.
  8. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 90 minutes.
  9. Add zucchini and bell pepper. Continue cooking for 20–25 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  10. Stir in the olives, parsley, cilantro, and lemon juice.
  11. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  12. Serve hot over couscous, quinoa, or cauliflower rice.
Protein: 38 g
Carbs: 18 g
Fat: 22 g
Saturated Fat: 6 g
Fiber: 5 g
Sugar: 10 g
Sodium: 525 mg
Cholesterol: 96 mg

Chef Tip

For even richer flavor, prepare the tagine a day ahead. The spices continue to develop overnight, making leftovers even more delicious.

16. Indian Chicken Curry

Bring the rich, comforting flavors of India to your table with this creamy, aromatic chicken curry. Tender chicken thighs simmer in a fragrant blend of spices, tomatoes, coconut milk, and fresh herbs for a satisfying meal that's perfect over basmati rice or warm naan.

  • 452 cal
  • Prep: 20 min
  • Cook: 40 min
  • Serves 6

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1½-inch pieces
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 medium jalapeño, seeded and finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 (13.5 oz) can full-fat coconut milk
  • ½ cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of ½ lime
  • For serving (optional): cooked basmati rice, warm naan, plain Greek yogurt, extra cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Pat the chicken dry with paper towels and season lightly with the salt and pepper.
  2. Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven or deep skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chicken in batches and brown for 3–4 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
  4. Reduce the heat to medium. Add the onion and sauté for 5–6 minutes until softened.
  5. Stir in the garlic, ginger, and jalapeño. Cook for 1 minute.
  6. Add the curry powder, garam masala, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, and cayenne. Stir constantly for 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  7. Mix in the tomato paste and cook for another minute.
  8. Pour in the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, and coconut milk. Stir until smooth.
  9. Return the browned chicken and any juices to the pan.
  10. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook for 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  11. Stir in the chopped spinach and cook for 2–3 minutes until wilted.
  12. Remove from the heat and stir in the cilantro and lime juice.
  13. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  14. Serve over hot basmati rice with naan and a dollop of Greek yogurt if desired.
Protein: 38 g
Carbs: 12 g
Fat: 28 g
Saturated Fat: 13 g
Fiber: 3 g
Sugar: 5 g
Sodium: 610 mg
Cholesterol: 148 mg
Potassium: 710 mg

Chef Tip

For the deepest flavor, prepare the curry a day ahead and refrigerate overnight. The spices continue to develop, making the curry even richer the next day. Garnish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime just before serving for the freshest finish.

17. Thai Lamb Roast

Experience the bold flavors of Thailand with this tender herb-crusted lamb roast infused with garlic, lemongrass, ginger, coconut milk, and fragrant Thai spices. Perfect for family dinners or special occasions.

  • 488 cal
  • Prep: 25 min
  • Cook: 1 hr 30 min
  • Serves 6

Ingredients

  • Lamb roast: 2½ lbs boneless leg of lamb
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemongrass, finely minced
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp lime zest
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Vegetables: 1 large red bell pepper, sliced; 1 large yellow bell pepper, sliced; 1 medium red onion, cut into wedges; 2 cups broccoli florets; 1 cup snow peas; 1 cup baby carrots
  • Thai seasoning blend: 2 tsp ground coriander, 1 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp paprika, ½ tsp white pepper, ½ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional), 1 tsp kosher salt
  • Coconut glaze: ½ cup full-fat coconut milk, 1 tbsp honey, 1 tbsp sweet chili sauce, 1 tsp red curry paste
  • Garnish: ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro, 2 tbsp chopped Thai basil, 2 green onions, sliced, lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Pat the lamb dry using paper towels.
  3. In a bowl, combine avocado oil, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, lemongrass, fish sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, lime zest, and lime juice.
  4. Rub the mixture evenly over the lamb.
  5. Mix together coriander, cumin, paprika, turmeric, salt, white pepper, and red pepper flakes.
  6. Coat the lamb evenly with the spice mixture.
  7. Arrange the onions, carrots, broccoli, bell peppers, and snow peas in a large roasting pan.
  8. Place the lamb on top of the vegetables.
  9. Roast uncovered for 60 minutes.
  10. Meanwhile, whisk together coconut milk, honey, sweet chili sauce, and curry paste.
  11. Brush the glaze over the lamb during the last 30 minutes of cooking.
  12. Continue roasting until the internal temperature reaches 135°F for medium-rare or 145°F for medium.
  13. Remove the lamb and let it rest for 15 minutes before slicing.
  14. Garnish with cilantro, Thai basil, and green onions.
  15. Serve with roasted vegetables and jasmine rice.
Protein: 41 g
Carbs: 14 g
Fat: 29 g
Saturated Fat: 10 g
Fiber: 4 g
Sugar: 7 g
Sodium: 545 mg
Cholesterol: 118 mg
Potassium: 810 mg

Chef Tip

Marinate the lamb overnight for maximum tenderness and flavor. Serve with steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice and drizzle any remaining coconut glaze over the sliced lamb before serving.

18. Japanese Pork Stew

Enjoy the comforting flavors of Japan with this hearty pork stew featuring tender pork shoulder, daikon radish, potatoes, carrots, mushrooms, and a savory soy-ginger broth. This slow-simmered dish is rich, satisfying, and perfect for cool evenings.

  • 465 cal
  • Prep: 20 min
  • Cook: 1 hr 40 min
  • Serves 6

Ingredients

  • Pork stew: 2 lbs boneless pork shoulder, cut into 1½-inch cubes
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 medium Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 cups daikon radish, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • Japanese seasoning: ¼ cup low-sodium soy sauce, 2 tbsp mirin, 2 tbsp cooking sake, 1 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp kosher salt, ½ tsp black pepper
  • Thickener: 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp cold water
  • Garnish: 3 green onions, thinly sliced; 2 tbsp toasted sesame seeds; 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (optional)

Instructions

  1. Pat the pork dry with paper towels and season lightly with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of sesame oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  3. Brown the pork in batches for about 6–8 minutes until golden on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add the onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened.
  5. Stir in the garlic and grated ginger; cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Return the pork to the pot.
  7. Add the potatoes, carrots, daikon, and mushrooms.
  8. Pour in the chicken broth and water.
  9. Stir in the soy sauce, mirin, sake, brown sugar, and remaining sesame oil.
  10. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  11. Cover and simmer for 1 hour 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  12. Mix the cornstarch with the cold water until smooth.
  13. Slowly stir the slurry into the stew and simmer another 5 minutes until slightly thickened.
  14. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  15. Garnish with green onions, sesame seeds, and parsley before serving.

Potassium: 940 mg

For the most authentic flavor, prepare the stew a few hours before serving and gently reheat it. The pork becomes even more tender, and the vegetables absorb the rich soy-ginger broth beautifully. Serve with steamed jasmine rice or traditional Japanese short-grain rice.
