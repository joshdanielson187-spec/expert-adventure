1. Salmon Avocado Plate

  • 560 cal
  • 40 g protein
  • 22 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (5–6 oz each)
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 4 cups mixed greens
  • 1 lemon (half juiced, half for wedges)
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp sea salt, 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • Optional: 1/2 tsp smoked paprika

Instructions

  1. Pat salmon dry. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Place salmon skin-down, sear 4 minutes until skin is crisp.
  3. Flip and cook 2–3 minutes more until flesh flakes easily with a fork.
  4. Toss greens with 1 tbsp olive oil and lemon juice; season lightly with salt.
  5. Plate greens, fan avocado slices, top with salmon, and serve with a lemon wedge.

Prep notes

You can sub roasted asparagus or steamed broccoli for the greens. Leftover salmon flakes great into a next-day salad.

2. Chickpea Power Salad

  • 460 cal
  • 22 g protein
  • 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 English cucumber, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely diced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, 2 tbsp lemon juice, 1 tsp Dijon mustard (gluten-free)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Whisk olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  2. Combine chickpeas, cucumber, pepper, red onion, feta, and parsley in a large bowl.
  3. Pour dressing over and toss gently.
  4. Chill 10 minutes before serving — flavor improves as it sits.

Prep notes

Holds 4 days in the fridge. Add protein (grilled chicken or tuna) to make it a heartier dinner.

3. Chicken Fajita Cauliflower Rice Bowl

  • 490 cal
  • 42 g protein
  • 25 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow onion, sliced
  • 3 cups cauliflower rice (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tbsp gluten-free fajita seasoning
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lime, juiced; fresh cilantro to garnish

Instructions

  1. Toss chicken slices with fajita seasoning and 1 tbsp olive oil.
  2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high. Cook chicken 5–6 minutes until browned and cooked through. Remove.
  3. Add remaining oil; sauté peppers and onion 4–5 minutes until tender-crisp.
  4. Return chicken to skillet. Toss to combine. Squeeze lime over the top.
  5. Warm cauliflower rice in microwave or skillet 2–3 minutes. Plate rice, top with fajita mix, garnish with cilantro.

Prep notes

Confirm your fajita seasoning is labeled gluten-free — many big-brand blends contain wheat.

4. Greek Yogurt Berry Bowl

  • 340 cal
  • 28 g protein
  • 5 min
  • Serves 1

Ingredients

  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt (2% or full-fat)
  • 1/2 cup mixed berries (blueberries, strawberries, raspberries)
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 2 tbsp sliced almonds
  • 1 tsp honey (optional)
  • Pinch of cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Spoon yogurt into a bowl.
  2. Top with berries, chia, almonds, and a drizzle of honey.
  3. Sprinkle cinnamon and serve chilled.

Prep notes

Confirm yogurt is plain (no added grain stabilizers) for a strict GF day. Skip granola unless certified gluten-free.

5. Turkey Taco Lettuce Cups

  • 410 cal
  • 38 g protein
  • 20 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lean ground turkey (93/7)
  • 2 tbsp gluten-free taco seasoning
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 large head romaine lettuce, separated into cups
  • 1 cup diced tomato
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Optional: chopped cilantro, hot sauce (GF)

Instructions

  1. Brown turkey in a dry skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up as it cooks (6–7 minutes).
  2. Sprinkle taco seasoning over the turkey, add water, stir, and simmer 2–3 minutes until thickened.
  3. Spoon turkey into romaine cups.
  4. Top with tomato, avocado, and a squeeze of lime. Add cilantro if desired.

Prep notes

Many store-bought taco seasonings include wheat — use a brand explicitly labeled gluten-free.

6. Cajun Cod with Green Beans

  • 380 cal
  • 36 g protein
  • 22 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 cod fillets (5–6 oz each)
  • 1 tbsp gluten-free Cajun seasoning
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 12 oz fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp salt, lemon wedges to serve

Instructions

  1. Pat cod dry and rub with Cajun seasoning.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Sear cod 3–4 minutes per side until flaky and opaque.
  3. In a separate pan, heat remaining oil. Add garlic and green beans; sauté 5–6 minutes until tender-crisp.
  4. Plate cod with green beans and a lemon wedge.

Prep notes

Tilapia or haddock work as 1:1 swaps. Check the Cajun blend label — wheat is a common hidden ingredient.

7. Apple Almond Protein Plate

  • 320 cal
  • 22 g protein
  • 5 min
  • Serves 1

Ingredients

  • 1 medium apple, sliced
  • 2 tbsp almond butter (no added sugar)
  • 1/4 cup raw almonds
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs OR 3/4 cup cottage cheese
  • Pinch of cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Slice apple into thin wedges.
  2. Plate apple with almond butter for dipping.
  3. Add almonds and your protein side (eggs or cottage cheese).
  4. Sprinkle cinnamon and enjoy.

Prep notes

This is a perfect "no-cook" lunch or snack. Cottage cheese should be plain — flavored varieties may include modified food starch.

8. Garlic Butter Chicken Skillet (GF)

  • 490 cal
  • 44 g protein
  • 20 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken cutlets (about 12 oz total)
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika, 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Season cutlets with paprika, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Sear chicken 2–3 minutes per side until cooked through. Remove.
  3. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter and garlic; cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Stir in honey. Return chicken; spoon sauce over the top. Garnish with parsley.

Prep notes

Serve over cauliflower rice or roasted potatoes — both naturally gluten-free.

9. Honey Garlic Shrimp Sheet Pan (GF)

  • 430 cal
  • 34 g protein
  • 20 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
  • 3 tbsp gluten-free tamari (or coconut aminos)
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Whisk tamari, honey, garlic, ginger, and olive oil.
  2. Toss shrimp and broccoli with two-thirds of the sauce on a sheet pan.
  3. Roast 8–10 minutes until shrimp are pink and broccoli is tender.
  4. Drizzle remaining sauce, sprinkle sesame seeds, and serve.

Prep notes

Soy sauce contains wheat — use tamari or coconut aminos to keep this strictly gluten-free.

10. Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry (Tamari)

  • 520 cal
  • 40 g protein
  • 25 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 12 oz flank or sirloin steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
  • 3 tbsp gluten-free tamari
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tsp cornstarch (verified GF) or arrowroot
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced; 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 2 tbsp avocado or olive oil

Instructions

  1. Whisk tamari, vinegar, honey, cornstarch, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok or large skillet over high. Stir-fry beef 2 minutes until just browned. Remove.
  3. Add remaining oil; stir-fry broccoli 3–4 minutes with a splash of water until tender-crisp.
  4. Return beef. Pour sauce over and toss until glossy and thickened (about 1 minute).
  5. Serve over rice or cauliflower rice.

Prep notes

Skip pre-mixed stir-fry sauces — most contain wheat. The whisked tamari sauce above is the safer GF version.

11. Pesto Zoodle Turkey Pasta

  • 450 cal
  • 36 g protein
  • 20 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey (93/7)
  • 4 medium zucchini, spiralized
  • 1/3 cup pesto (verify GF — many use breadcrumbs)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, red pepper flakes

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Brown turkey, breaking it up, 6–7 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Add cherry tomatoes; cook 2 minutes until they soften.
  3. Add zoodles and pesto. Toss 2 minutes until zoodles are just tender (don't overcook).
  4. Top with parmesan and red pepper flakes.

Prep notes

Read pesto labels — some include breadcrumbs or wheat fillers. Homemade or a clean GF brand is safest.

12. Buffalo Chicken Stuffed Peppers

  • 510 cal
  • 42 g protein
  • 35 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 4 bell peppers, tops cut, seeds removed
  • 1 lb cooked shredded chicken (rotisserie works)
  • 1/3 cup gluten-free buffalo sauce
  • 1/2 cup cooked rice or cauliflower rice
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar
  • 2 tbsp ranch or blue-cheese dressing (GF)
  • 2 tbsp green onion, sliced

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Place peppers in a baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, mix shredded chicken, buffalo sauce, and rice.
  3. Stuff peppers, top with cheddar.
  4. Bake 22–25 minutes until peppers soften and cheese melts.
  5. Drizzle with ranch or blue cheese; garnish with green onion.

Prep notes

Frank's RedHot original is gluten-free; verify your dressing brand too. Skip the rice for a lower-carb version.
