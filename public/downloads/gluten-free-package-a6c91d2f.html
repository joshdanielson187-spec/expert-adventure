1. Salmon Avocado Plate

  • 560 cal
  • Prep: 5 min
  • Cook: 10 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (5–6 oz each)
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced (½ avocado per serving)
  • 4 cups mixed greens (2 cups per serving)
  • 1 lemon (½ juiced for dressing, ½ for wedges)
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • Optional: ½ tsp smoked paprika

Instructions

  1. Pat salmon dry. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Place salmon skin-down, sear 4 minutes until skin is crisp.
  3. Flip and cook 2–3 minutes more until flesh flakes easily with a fork.
  4. Toss greens with 1 tbsp olive oil and lemon juice; season lightly with salt.
  5. Plate greens, fan avocado slices, top with salmon, and serve with a lemon wedge.
Protein: 40 g
Carbs: 12 g
Fat: 28 g
Fiber: 4 g

Gluten-Free Notes

This recipe is naturally gluten-free. All ingredients are whole foods with no wheat, barley, or rye. Verify salmon source if concerned about cross-contamination.

Meal Prep Notes

You can sub roasted asparagus or steamed broccoli for the greens. Leftover salmon flakes great into a next-day salad. Store components separately and assemble fresh.

2. Lemon Herb Salmon Bowl

  • 580 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 1½ cups cooked quinoa (¾ cup per serving)
  • 1 cup roasted broccoli florets (½ cup per serving)
  • ½ cup diced cucumber
  • ½ cup diced tomato
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill (or ½ tsp dried)
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

Instructions

  1. Season salmon with salt, pepper, and dill. Sear in 1 tbsp oil over medium-high heat, 4–5 min per side, until cooked through.
  2. Divide cooked quinoa between two bowls. Top with salmon fillet.
  3. Arrange roasted broccoli, cucumber, and tomato around each bowl.
  4. Whisk together 1 tbsp oil and lemon juice. Drizzle over bowls.
Protein: 42 g
Carbs: 38 g
Fat: 24 g
Fiber: 6 g

Gluten-Free Notes

Quinoa is naturally gluten-free. Verify all processed ingredients (dill) are certified GF to avoid cross-contamination.

Meal Prep Notes

Cook quinoa and broccoli on Sunday. Store separately. Assemble fresh or reheat gently. Stores 3 days in refrigerator.

3. Chickpea Power Salad

  • 460 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 0 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, rinsed and drained (1½ cups)
  • 1 English cucumber, diced (2 cups)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced (1 cup)
  • ½ red onion, finely diced (½ cup)
  • ½ cup crumbled feta
  • ¼ cup chopped parsley
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard (gluten-free)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Whisk olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  2. Combine chickpeas, cucumber, pepper, red onion, feta, and parsley in a large bowl.
  3. Pour dressing over and toss gently.
  4. Chill 10 minutes before serving — flavor improves as it sits.
Protein: 16 g
Carbs: 38 g
Fat: 16 g
Fiber: 10 g

Gluten-Free Notes

Verify Dijon mustard is certified gluten-free. Most major brands are, but always check labels. Chickpeas, vegetables, and feta are naturally gluten-free.

Meal Prep Notes

Holds 4 days in the fridge. Add protein (grilled chicken or tuna) to make it a heartier dinner. Perfect for meal prep Sunday.

4. Chicken Fajita Cauliflower Rice Bowl

  • 490 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 18 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (7 oz each), thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into strips (1 cup)
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced into strips (1 cup)
  • 1 yellow onion, sliced into strips (1 cup)
  • 3 cups cauliflower rice (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tbsp gluten-free fajita seasoning
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro to garnish

Instructions

  1. Toss chicken slices with fajita seasoning and 1 tbsp olive oil.
  2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high. Cook chicken 5–6 minutes until browned and cooked through. Remove.
  3. Add remaining oil; sauté peppers and onion 4–5 minutes until tender-crisp.
  4. Return chicken to skillet. Toss to combine. Squeeze lime over the top.
  5. Warm cauliflower rice in microwave or skillet 2–3 minutes. Plate rice, top with fajita mix, garnish with cilantro.
Protein: 42 g
Carbs: 16 g
Fat: 14 g
Fiber: 4 g

Gluten-Free Notes

CRITICAL: Confirm your fajita seasoning is labeled gluten-free — many big-brand blends contain wheat fillers. Purchase brands explicitly certified GF.

Meal Prep Notes

Slice peppers and onions on Sunday. Store separately from chicken. Assemble fresh or reheat gently to prevent mushiness.

6. Turkey Taco Lettuce Cups

  • 410 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 12 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lean ground turkey (93/7)
  • 2 tbsp gluten-free taco seasoning
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 large head romaine lettuce, separated into cups (8–10 leaves)
  • 1 cup diced tomato (1 large tomato)
  • 1 avocado, diced (½ avocado per serving)
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Optional: chopped cilantro, hot sauce (gluten-free)

Instructions

  1. Brown turkey in a dry skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up as it cooks (6–7 minutes).
  2. Sprinkle taco seasoning over the turkey, add water, stir, and simmer 2–3 minutes until thickened.
  3. Spoon turkey into romaine cups.
  4. Top with tomato, avocado, and a squeeze of lime. Add cilantro if desired.
Protein: 38 g
Carbs: 14 g
Fat: 18 g
Fiber: 4 g

Gluten-Free Notes

CRITICAL: Many store-bought taco seasonings include wheat — use a brand explicitly labeled gluten-free. Frank's RedHot is GF. Verify all sauces.

Meal Prep Notes

Cook turkey mixture and store in container for 4 days. Assemble lettuce cups fresh to prevent sogginess.

7. Cajun Cod with Green Beans

  • 380 cal
  • Prep: 5 min
  • Cook: 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 cod fillets (5–6 oz each)
  • 1 tbsp gluten-free Cajun seasoning
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 12 oz fresh green beans, trimmed (about 3 cups)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Lemon wedges to serve

Instructions

  1. Pat cod dry and rub with Cajun seasoning.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Sear cod 3–4 minutes per side until flaky and opaque.
  3. In a separate pan, heat remaining oil. Add garlic and green beans; sauté 5–6 minutes until tender-crisp.
  4. Plate cod with green beans and a lemon wedge.
Protein: 36 g
Carbs: 12 g
Fat: 10 g
Fiber: 3 g

Gluten-Free Notes

Check the Cajun blend label — wheat is a common hidden ingredient. Purchase brands explicitly certified gluten-free. Tilapia or haddock work as 1:1 swaps.

Meal Prep Notes

Trim green beans on Sunday and store in containers. Cod is best cooked fresh but can be stored 2 days raw.

9. Garlic Butter Chicken Skillet (GF)

  • 490 cal
  • Prep: 5 min
  • Cook: 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken cutlets (about 12 oz total)
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Season cutlets with paprika, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Sear chicken 2–3 minutes per side until cooked through. Remove.
  3. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter and garlic; cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Stir in honey. Return chicken; spoon sauce over the top. Garnish with parsley.
Protein: 44 g
Carbs: 8 g
Fat: 22 g
Fiber: 0 g

Gluten-Free Notes

This recipe is naturally gluten-free. Verify paprika and thyme are not from cross-contaminated sources. Serve over cauliflower rice or roasted potatoes — both naturally gluten-free.

Meal Prep Notes

Cook chicken and store in airtight container for 4 days. Reheat gently. Sauce is best made fresh.

10. Honey Garlic Shrimp Sheet Pan (GF)

  • 430 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 12 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb large shrimp (about 16–20 count), peeled and deveined
  • 1 head broccoli, cut into florets (4 cups)
  • 3 tbsp gluten-free tamari (or coconut aminos)
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Whisk tamari, honey, garlic, ginger, and olive oil.
  2. Toss shrimp and broccoli with two-thirds of the sauce on a sheet pan.
  3. Roast 8–10 minutes until shrimp are pink and broccoli is tender.
  4. Drizzle remaining sauce, sprinkle sesame seeds, and serve.
Protein: 34 g
Carbs: 20 g
Fat: 8 g
Fiber: 3 g

Gluten-Free Notes

CRITICAL: Soy sauce contains wheat — use tamari or coconut aminos to keep this strictly gluten-free. Both are certified GF alternatives.

Meal Prep Notes

Prepare sauce and cut broccoli the night before. Peeled shrimp store for 2 days raw. Best eaten fresh from the oven.

11. Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry (Tamari)

  • 520 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 12 oz flank or sirloin steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 1 head broccoli, cut into florets (4 cups)
  • 3 tbsp gluten-free tamari
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tsp cornstarch (verified GF) or arrowroot
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 2 tbsp avocado or olive oil

Instructions

  1. Whisk tamari, vinegar, honey, cornstarch, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok or large skillet over high. Stir-fry beef 2 minutes until just browned. Remove.
  3. Add remaining oil; stir-fry broccoli 3–4 minutes with a splash of water until tender-crisp.
  4. Return beef. Pour sauce over and toss until glossy and thickened (about 1 minute).
  5. Serve over rice or cauliflower rice.
Protein: 40 g
Carbs: 14 g
Fat: 14 g
Fiber: 3 g

Gluten-Free Notes

Skip pre-mixed stir-fry sauces — most contain wheat. Verify tamari is certified gluten-free. Use arrowroot or cornstarch (verified GF). Rice vinegar is naturally GF.

Meal Prep Notes

Slice beef and chop broccoli ahead of time. Store separately. Stir-fry is best made fresh but can be reheated gently.

12. Pesto Zoodle Turkey Pasta

  • 450 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 12 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey (93/7)
  • 4 medium zucchini, spiralized
  • ⅓ cup pesto (verify GF — many use breadcrumbs)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, red pepper flakes

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Brown turkey, breaking it up, 6–7 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Add cherry tomatoes; cook 2 minutes until they soften.
  3. Add zoodles and pesto. Toss 2 minutes until zoodles are just tender (don't overcook).
  4. Top with parmesan and red pepper flakes.
Protein: 36 g
Carbs: 14 g
Fat: 16 g
Fiber: 3 g

Gluten-Free Notes

CRITICAL: Read pesto labels — some include breadcrumbs or wheat fillers. Homemade or a clean GF brand is safest. Most major brands now offer GF versions.

Meal Prep Notes

Spiralize zucchini up to 1 day ahead; store in a colander with paper towels to drain. Cook turkey ahead. Assemble fresh to prevent mushiness.

14. Mediterranean Chicken Power Bowl

  • 540 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 25 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (6–7 oz each), grilled or pan-seared
  • 1 cup cooked brown rice (½ cup per serving)
  • 1 cup diced cucumber
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

Instructions

Meal Prep Notes

Cook rice and chicken ahead on Sunday. Assemble fresh to preserve texture, or store components separately for 3 days and assemble on eating day.

15. Thai Gluten-Free Coconut Curry

  • 525 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 25 min
  • Serves 4

Creamy coconut curry with chicken, vegetables, basil, and jasmine rice.

Ingredients

  • 1½ lb boneless skinless chicken breast, cubed
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup sliced carrots
  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk, 13.5 oz
  • 2 tbsp gluten-free red curry paste
  • 1 tbsp gluten-free tamari
  • 1 tsp coconut sugar
  • 1 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • ¼ cup chopped Thai basil
  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice

Instructions

  1. Heat avocado oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add chicken and cook 5–6 minutes until lightly browned.
  3. Add onion, garlic, and ginger; cook 2 minutes.
  4. Add bell pepper, broccoli, and carrots; cook 5 minutes.
  5. Stir in curry paste, coconut milk, tamari, coconut sugar, and lime juice.
  6. Simmer 10–12 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F.
  7. Stir in Thai basil and serve over jasmine rice.
Protein: 36 g
Carbs: 31 g
Fat: 27 g
Fiber: 5 g
Sugar: 8 g
Sodium: 615 mg

Pantry Swaps

  • Protein can be swapped with chicken, shrimp, turkey, tofu, or beans when appropriate.
  • Rice can be swapped with quinoa, brown rice, or cauliflower rice.
  • Olive oil and avocado oil can be used interchangeably.
  • Use certified gluten-free sauces, curry paste, tamari, broth, and spice blends.

Meal Prep Notes

Prepare protein, vegetables, and grains ahead of time. Store cooked portions in airtight containers for up to 4 days. Reheat until hot. Most cooked portions can be frozen for up to 2 months.

20. Turkish Mango Chicken Stir Fry

  • 455 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 18 min
  • Serves 4

Sweet and savory chicken stir fry with mango, vegetables, sumac, and Aleppo pepper.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced thin
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 tbsp gluten-free tamari
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 tsp Aleppo pepper
  • ½ tsp sumac
  • ½ tsp cumin
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 2 tbsp chopped mint

Instructions

  1. Heat avocado oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Cook chicken 5–6 minutes until browned.
