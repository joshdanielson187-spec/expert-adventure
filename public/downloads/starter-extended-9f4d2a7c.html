15. Moroccan Beef Tagine

  • 620 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 45 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs beef chuck, cubed (¾-inch pieces)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced (1½ cups)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef broth (low-sodium)
  • 1 cup diced sweet potato (¾-inch cubes)
  • ½ cup dried apricots, halved
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • ¼ cup sliced raw almonds

Instructions

  1. Pat beef dry with paper towels. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  3. Brown beef in batches (about 4–5 min per batch), avoiding overcrowding. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to pot. Sauté diced onion 3–4 min until softened. Add minced garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Stir in tomato paste and cook 1 minute to caramelize slightly.
  6. Return beef to pot. Add cumin, ginger, cinnamon, and cayenne pepper. Stir to coat.
  7. Pour beef broth over beef. Bring to a simmer, cover, and reduce heat to medium-low.
  8. Simmer for 25 minutes. Add diced sweet potato and dried apricots. Continue simmering 15–20 min more until beef is tender and sweet potato is cooked through.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
  10. Ladle into bowls. Garnish with fresh cilantro and sliced almonds. Serve warm.
Protein: 48 g
Carbs: 42 g
Fat: 18 g
Fiber: 6 g

Meal Prep Notes

Tagine reheats beautifully and tastes even better the next day as flavors meld. Stores 5 days refrigerated. Serve over couscous or cauliflower rice. Freeze-friendly for up to 3 months.

16. Indian Chicken Curry

  • 540 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 30 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs chicken breast, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tbsp ghee or coconut oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced (1 cup)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, no sugar added
  • ½ cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup chicken broth (low-sodium)
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1.5 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper (or to taste)
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Season chicken cubes with ¼ tsp salt and ⅛ tsp black pepper.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp ghee in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear chicken in batches (3–4 min per batch) until golden on outside. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add remaining 1 tbsp ghee to skillet. Sauté diced onion 4–5 min until translucent.
  4. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Stir in cumin, coriander, turmeric, cayenne, and garam masala. Toast spices for 30 seconds, stirring constantly.
  6. Add diced tomatoes (with juice) and chicken broth. Bring to a simmer.
  7. Return chicken to skillet. Simmer uncovered for 15–18 min until chicken is cooked through (165°F) and sauce thickens slightly.
  8. Remove from heat. Stir in Greek yogurt and fresh lemon juice until well combined. Do not boil after adding yogurt.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or cayenne as needed.
  10. Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve over cauliflower rice or basmati rice.
Protein: 52 g
Carbs: 18 g
Fat: 16 g
Fiber: 3 g

Meal Prep Notes

Curry is a classic meal-prep dish. Stores 4 days in fridge. Reheat gently on stovetop or microwave (1.5–2 min). Freeze 3 months. Serve over rice, quinoa, or cauliflower rice.

17. Thai Lamb Roast with Coconut Rice

  • 680 cal
  • Prep: 20 min
  • Cook: 40 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1.25 lbs lamb loin chops or leg cubes
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 tbsp red curry paste (or 1 tsp red curry powder)
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) light coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (or low-sodium tamari)
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar or honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces (1 cup)
  • 1 cup cubed eggplant (¾-inch)
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 cup cooked jasmine or basmati rice (½ cup per serving)
  • ¼ cup fresh Thai basil (or regular basil), torn
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Pat lamb dry. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp coconut oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Sear lamb until browned on all sides (4–5 min total). Remove and set aside.
  3. Add remaining 1 tbsp coconut oil to skillet. Add red curry paste and cook 1 minute, stirring constantly.
  4. Add minced garlic and ginger. Cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Pour in coconut milk slowly, stirring to combine with curry paste. Add fish sauce and palm sugar. Stir well.
  6. Return lamb to skillet. Bring sauce to a gentle simmer, cover, and reduce heat to medium.
  7. Simmer 20 minutes. Add red bell pepper and eggplant cubes. Continue simmering 10 min.
  8. Add snap peas. Simmer 3–4 min more until vegetables are tender-crisp and lamb is cooked through.
  9. Remove from heat. Stir in fresh lime juice and torn Thai basil.
  10. Divide cooked rice between two bowls. Ladle curry over rice and serve immediately.
Protein: 44 g
Carbs: 48 g
Fat: 24 g
Fiber: 5 g

Meal Prep Notes

Thai curries reheat beautifully. Store 4 days in fridge. Freeze rice and curry separately for up to 3 months. Assemble fresh for best flavor.

18. Japanese Pork Stew

  • 580 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 50 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs pork shoulder, cubed (¾-inch)
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil, divided
  • 1 medium yellow onion, quartered (1 cup)
  • 3 medium carrots, cut into 1-inch pieces (1.5 cups)
  • 8 oz daikon radish, cut into 1-inch cubes (1.5 cups)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • ½ cup sake or dry white wine
  • 2 cups pork or chicken broth (low-sodium)
  • 3 tbsp low-sodium tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin (or 1 tsp honey)
  • 1 tsp ground ginger (dried)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Pat pork dry. Season with ¼ tsp salt and ⅛ tsp black pepper.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp sesame oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown pork in batches (4–5 min per batch). Remove and set aside.
  3. Add remaining 1 tbsp sesame oil. Sauté onion 3–4 min until softened. Add garlic and fresh ginger; cook 1 minute.
  4. Pour sake into pot, scraping up browned bits (fond) from the bottom. Let simmer 2 minutes to reduce slightly.
  5. Add pork broth, tamari, mirin, dried ginger, and bay leaf. Return pork to pot.
  6. Bring to a simmer, cover, reduce heat to medium-low. Simmer 25 minutes.
  7. Add carrots and daikon radish cubes. Continue simmering 20–25 min until vegetables are tender and pork is cooked through.
  8. Remove bay leaf. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or additional tamari.
  9. Ladle into bowls. Garnish with sliced green onions and sesame seeds. Serve hot.
Protein: 46 g
Carbs: 28 g
Fat: 20 g
Fiber: 5 g

Meal Prep Notes

Japanese stews improve with time as flavors deepen. Stores 5 days. Freezes 3 months. Reheat gently on stovetop. Great for meal prep Sunday.

19. Korean Duck Skewers with Gochujang

  • 520 cal
  • Prep: 20 min
  • Cook: 20 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb duck breast, cubed (¾-inch)
  • 8 wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 min
  • 3 tbsp gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp low-sodium tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together gochujang, tamari, rice vinegar, honey, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger until smooth. Reserve 3 tbsp for serving.
  2. Pat duck cubes dry. Season with ¼ tsp salt and ⅛ tsp black pepper.
  3. Thread duck cubes onto soaked wooden skewers (about 4–5 pieces per skewer).
  4. Brush skewers with gochujang marinade, coating all sides.
  5. Heat a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat until hot.
  6. Place skewers in pan. Cook 4–5 min per side, brushing with remaining marinade, until duck is cooked through (165°F internal temp) and caramelized on the outside.
  7. Transfer skewers to a serving plate. Drizzle with reserved gochujang sauce.
  8. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions. Serve with rice or roasted vegetables.
Protein: 40 g
Carbs: 12 g
Fat: 22 g
Fiber: 1 g

Meal Prep Notes

Marinate duck cubes up to 4 hours ahead. Cook fresh for best texture. Can be eaten cold as a next-day snack or reheated gently.

20. Brazilian Venison Rice Bowl

  • 640 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 35 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs venison steak or loin, sliced thin (¼-inch)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced (1.5 cups)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced (1 cup)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup cooked white or brown rice
  • 1 cup diced tomato (1 large tomato)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Pat venison slices dry. Season with ¼ tsp salt and ⅛ tsp black pepper.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until very hot.
  3. Sear venison slices in batches (2–3 min per side). Venison is best served medium-rare to avoid toughness. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to skillet. Sauté diced onion 4–5 min until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.
  5. Add red bell pepper. Sauté 3–4 min until tender-crisp.
  6. Stir in tomato paste, cumin, and paprika. Cook 1 minute to toast spices.
  7. Add diced tomato and black beans. Simmer 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Return venison to skillet along with any accumulated juices. Simmer 2–3 min to warm through. Do not overcook or venison will become tough.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  10. Divide rice between two bowls. Top with venison and bean mixture. Garnish with cilantro and serve with lime wedge.
Protein: 50 g
Carbs: 48 g
Fat: 16 g
Fiber: 8 g

Meal Prep Notes

Venison is lean and best enjoyed fresh or quickly reheated. Cook rice and bean mixture ahead; add sliced venison fresh to prevent overcooking. Stores 3 days.

21. Mexican Goat Carnitas Wrap

  • 520 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 2 hours (low/slow)
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs goat shoulder or leg, cut into 2-inch chunks
  • 3 tbsp lard or avocado oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, quartered
  • 6 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 whole wheat wraps (10-inch)
  • 1 avocado, sliced (½ per wrap)
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • ½ cup diced tomato
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Hot sauce to taste (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F. Pat goat meat dry and season with ½ tsp salt and ¼ tsp black pepper.
  2. Heat lard in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown goat chunks in batches (3–4 min per batch). Remove and set aside.
  3. Add quartered onion to pot. Sauté 2 minutes. Add garlic cloves and cook 1 minute more.
  4. Return goat to pot. Add bay leaves, oregano, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Toss to coat.
  5. Cover pot with a lid. Transfer to oven and braise for 1.5–2 hours until goat is very tender and easily shreds with a fork.
  6. Remove from oven. Shred goat meat with two forks, discarding large bones. Discard bay leaves. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  7. Warm wraps in microwave or skillet (30 seconds per side).
  8. Spread shredded goat carnitas down the center of each wrap. Top with avocado slices, shredded cabbage, diced tomato, and cilantro.
  9. Fold wrap tightly. Serve with lime wedge and hot sauce on the side.
Protein: 46 g
Carbs: 32 g
Fat: 20 g
Fiber: 6 g

Meal Prep Notes

Carnitas are perfect for meal prep! Cook ahead and store shredded goat for 5 days in fridge. Reheat gently on stovetop or microwave. Freezes 3 months. Assemble wraps fresh.

22. Greek Bison Burger Bowl

  • 480 cal
  • Prep: 12 min
  • Cook: 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground bison (93/7 or leaner)
  • ¼ cup finely diced red onion
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • ¼ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cups mixed greens or romaine lettuce
  • ½ cup diced cucumber
  • ½ cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ¼ cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (for dressing)
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar or lemon juice

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine ground bison, red onion, parsley, dill, garlic, oregano, ¼ cup feta, salt, and pepper. Mix gently with your hands until just combined. Do not overwork the meat.
  2. Form into 2 burger patties (about 4 oz each, ¾-inch thick).
  3. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Cook burger patties 6–7 min per side for medium doneness (160°F internal temp). Avoid pressing down on patties as they cook.
  5. While patties cook, prepare salad: Combine mixed greens, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and olives in a large bowl.
  6. Whisk together 2 tbsp olive oil and red wine vinegar. Drizzle over greens and toss gently.
  7. Divide dressed greens between two bowls or plates.
  8. Top each portion with a cooked bison burger patty. Garnish with extra feta and olives.
  9. Serve immediately.
Protein: 40 g
Carbs: 12 g
Fat: 22 g
Fiber: 3 g

Meal Prep Notes

Form patties ahead and refrigerate up to 24 hours, or freeze for 3 months. Cook fresh for best texture. Bison is very lean; avoid overcooking to prevent drying out.

23. Turkish Elk Braise with Pomegranate

  • 600 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 55 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs elk shoulder or leg, cubed (¾-inch)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced (1.5 cups)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1.5 cups beef broth (low-sodium)
  • ¼ cup pomegranate molasses (or 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar + 1 tbsp honey)
  • 1 cup diced eggplant (¾-inch cubes)
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces (1 cup)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp red pepper flakes
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tbsp pomegranate seeds (optional garnish)

Instructions

  1. Pat elk dry. Season with ¼ tsp salt and ⅛ tsp black pepper.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Brown elk in batches (4–5 min per batch). Remove and set aside.
  3. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil. Sauté diced onion 4–5 min until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.
  4. Stir in tomato paste. Cook 1 minute to caramelize.
  5. Add cumin, cinnamon, red pepper flakes, and cloves. Stir constantly for 30 seconds to toast spices.
  6. Return elk to pot. Pour in beef broth and pomegranate molasses. Bring to a simmer.
  7. Cover and reduce heat to medium-low. Simmer 30 minutes.
  8. Add diced eggplant and red bell pepper. Continue simmering 20–25 min until elk is very tender and vegetables are cooked through.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or additional pomegranate molasses.
  10. Ladle into bowls. Garnish with fresh parsley and pomegranate seeds. Serve warm.
Protein: 48 g
Carbs: 28 g
Fat: 18 g
Fiber: 5 g

Meal Prep Notes

Braised elk improves as flavors meld. Stores 5 days in fridge. Freezes beautifully for 3 months. Reheat gently on stovetop. Serve over rice, couscous, or with roasted vegetables.

24. Ethiopian Turkey Fusion Stew

  • 480 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 40 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs ground turkey (93/7)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or coconut oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced (1.5 cups)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, no sugar added
  • ½ cup beef broth (low-sodium)
  • 1 tsp paprika (preferably smoked or Hungarian)
  • 1 tsp ground fenugreek (optional but authentic)
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (or to taste)
  • ¼ tsp turmeric powder
  • ⅛ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tbsp ghee in a large pot over medium-high heat. Brown ground turkey, breaking it up as it cooks (6–7 min). Remove and set aside.
  2. Add remaining 1 tbsp ghee to pot. Sauté diced onion 5–6 min until deeply caramelized (this develops sweet depth of flavor). Add garlic and ginger; cook 1 minute.
  3. Stir in tomato paste. Cook 2 minutes to concentrate and caramelize slightly.
  4. Add paprika, fenugreek, cumin, coriander, cayenne, turmeric, and cloves. Toast spices, stirring constantly, for 1 minute.
  5. Add diced tomatoes (with juice) and beef broth. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Return ground turkey to pot. Stir well.
  7. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer uncovered for 25–30 min, stirring occasionally, until sauce thickens and flavors deepen.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or additional cayenne as desired.
  9. Remove from heat. Stir in fresh lemon juice and cilantro.
  10. Serve hot over rice, millet, or with injera bread (Ethiopian flatbread).
Protein: 44 g
Carbs: 20 g
Fat: 16 g
Fiber: 4 g

Meal Prep Notes

Ethiopian stew freezes excellently for 3 months. Stores 5 days refrigerated. Reheat on stovetop for best flavor. Better the next day as spices meld.

25. Spanish Chorizo Sheet Pan

  • 520 cal
  • Prep: 12 min
  • Cook: 30 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 10 oz Spanish chorizo sausage, sliced into ¼-inch coins
  • 1 large red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces (1.5 cups)
  • 1 large yellow bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces (1.5 cups)
  • 1 large yellow onion, cut into 1-inch pieces (1.5 cups)
  • 2 cups baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine red pepper, yellow pepper, onion, and baby potatoes.
  3. Drizzle with 2 tbsp olive oil. Add minced garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Toss vegetables until evenly coated with oil and spices.
  5. Spread vegetables on prepared sheet pan in a single layer.
  6. Roast for 15 minutes until vegetables begin to soften.
  7. Remove pan from oven. Scatter chorizo slices evenly over vegetables.
  8. Return to oven and roast for 12–15 min more until chorizo is heated through and vegetables are tender and caramelized on the edges.
  9. Transfer to a serving platter. Sprinkle with fresh parsley. Serve with lemon wedges.
Protein: 24 g
Carbs: 32 g
Fat: 28 g
Fiber: 4 g

Meal Prep Notes

Sheet pan meals are excellent for meal prep. Stores 4 days in fridge. Reheat at 350°F until warmed through. Can be eaten cold as a next-day salad over greens.

26. Vietnamese Pork Meatballs

  • 480 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 20 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork (80/20 blend)
  • ¼ cup finely minced fresh cilantro
  • 2 tbsp finely minced fresh mint
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 2 tbsp gluten-free panko breadcrumbs (or crushed rice crackers)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (or low-sodium tamari)
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp avocado or coconut oil
  • 2 cups lettuce leaves or mixed greens
  • 1 cup julienned vegetables (carrots, cucumber, bell pepper)
  • Fresh herbs for serving (cilantro, mint, Thai basil)

Nuoc Cham Dipping Sauce

  • 3 tbsp fish sauce (or tamari)
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ½ Thai chili, minced (or ¼ tsp red pepper flakes)
  • 2 tbsp water

Instructions (Meatballs)

  1. In a bowl, combine ground pork, cilantro, mint, garlic, lime juice, breadcrumbs, egg, fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix gently with hands until just combined. Do not overwork.
  2. Form into 16 meatballs (about 1 oz each). Place on a parchment-lined plate.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Working in batches, cook meatballs 4–5 min, turning occasionally, until cooked through (160°F internal temp). Remove to a plate.

Instructions (Nuoc Cham Sauce)

  1. Whisk together fish sauce, lime juice, honey, garlic, Thai chili, and water in a small bowl. Taste and adjust seasonings.

Instructions (Assembly)

  1. Arrange lettuce leaves and julienned vegetables on a platter.
  2. Top with cooked meatballs. Scatter fresh herbs around.
  3. Serve with small bowl of nuoc cham sauce for dipping. Guests can roll meatballs in lettuce with herbs and dip in sauce.
Protein: 36 g
Carbs: 14 g
Fat: 24 g
Fiber: 2 g

Meal Prep Notes

Form meatballs ahead and refrigerate up to 24 hours or freeze for 3 months. Cook fresh or reheat gently in oven at 350°F for 8–10 min. Perfect for appetizers or meal prep bowls.

27. Portuguese Chicken Marinade

  • 480 cal
  • Prep: 15 min + 4 hours marinating
  • Cook: 25 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients (Marinade)

  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh cilantro, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper (or to taste for piri piri heat)
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

Ingredients (Chicken)

  • 1.5 lbs chicken thighs and drumsticks (or 2 chicken breasts), skin-on preferred
  • 1 tbsp avocado oil (for cooking)
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together garlic, parsley, cilantro, paprika, cayenne, red wine vinegar, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper to make marinade.
  2. Pat chicken dry. Place in a large resealable bag or bowl.
  3. Pour marinade over chicken, ensuring all pieces are well coated. Massage marinade into meat and skin.
  4. Seal bag or cover bowl. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. Can marinate up to 24 hours.
  5. Remove chicken from refrigerator 20 minutes before cooking to bring to room temperature.
  6. Heat 1 tbsp avocado oil in a large skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat.
  7. Place marinated chicken skin-side down in pan. Cook 10–12 min until skin is golden and crispy.
  8. Flip chicken. Cook 10–12 min more until cooked through (165°F internal temp for thighs, 160°F for breasts).
  9. Transfer chicken to a serving plate. Drizzle with pan juices. Garnish with fresh parsley. Serve with lemon wedges.
Protein: 42 g
Carbs: 4 g
Fat: 24 g
Fiber: 0 g

Meal Prep Notes

Marinate chicken up to 24 hours ahead. Cook fresh for best texture. Leftover cooked chicken can be stored 4 days and enjoyed cold or reheated. Great for meal prep bowls.

28. Italian Beef Ragu

  • 560 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 1 hour 15 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs beef chuck, finely diced (¼-inch)
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely diced (1.5 cups)
  • 2 medium carrots, finely diced (1 cup)
  • 2 celery stalks, finely diced (¾ cup) — this is called "soffritto" in Italian cooking
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • ½ cup whole milk (or heavy cream for richer sauce)
  • 1 cup beef broth (low-sodium)
  • ¼ cup red wine (optional, or use additional broth)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown beef in batches (4–5 min per batch), stirring occasionally. Remove and set aside.
  2. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to pot. Sauté soffritto (onion, carrot, celery) over medium heat for 8–10 min until vegetables soften and develop color. Add garlic; cook 1 minute.
  3. Stir in tomato paste. Cook 2 minutes to deepen color and flavor.
  4. Add crushed tomatoes (with juice) and red wine if using. Scrape up any browned bits (fond) from the bottom of pot.
  5. Return beef to pot along with beef broth, bay leaves, oregano, thyme, salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  6. Bring to a gentle simmer. Partially cover pot (leave lid slightly ajar to allow some evaporation).
  7. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 50–60 min, stirring occasionally, until sauce thickens and beef is very tender.
  8. Stir in milk (or cream). Simmer 5 minutes more.
  9. Remove bay leaves. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional salt, pepper, or nutmeg.
  10. Serve over zucchini noodles, cauliflower rice, or traditional pasta. Garnish with fresh parsley and Parmesan cheese if desired.

Carbs: 26 g
Fat: 20 g
Fiber: 4 g

Meal Prep Notes

Ragu is a quintessential meal-prep dish. Improves as it sits; flavors deepen overnight. Stores 5 days in fridge, freezes 3 months. Reheat gently on stovetop. Serve over various bases for variety.

29. French Herb Lamb Chops

  • 520 cal
  • Prep: 10 min
  • Cook: 12 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 4 lamb rib chops (about 12 oz total), 1 to 1.5 inches thick
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • ¼ cup finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp panko breadcrumbs (or almond flour for lower carb)
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp avocado or olive oil
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • 2 cups roasted or steamed vegetables (asparagus, green beans, or broccoli)

Instructions

  1. Pat lamb chops dry. Season both sides with ¼ tsp salt and ⅛ tsp black pepper.
  2. In a small bowl, mix Dijon mustard, minced garlic, rosemary, and thyme.
  3. In another small bowl, combine Parmesan, breadcrumbs, ¼ tsp salt, and ⅛ tsp pepper.
  4. Brush one side of each lamb chop with mustard-herb mixture. Press herb-Parmesan mixture onto mustard side, coating evenly.
  5. Heat 2 tbsp avocado oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.
  6. Place chops herb-side up in skillet. Cook 5–6 min without moving them until golden crust forms on bottom.
  7. Gently flip chops. Cook 4–6 min more for medium-rare (130–135°F internal temp). Do not overcook or lamb will become tough.
  8. Transfer chops to serving plates. Serve immediately with roasted vegetables and lemon wedges.
Protein: 40 g
Carbs: 8 g
Fat: 28 g
Fiber: 1 g

Meal Prep Notes

Best eaten fresh for optimal texture and flavor. Lamb chops are quick-cooking, perfect for weeknight dinners. Prep herb-Parmesan crust ahead and coat chops just before cooking.

30. Swedish Meatball Power Bowl

  • 540 cal
  • Prep: 15 min
  • Cook: 30 min
  • Serves 2

Ingredients (Meatballs)

  • 1 lb ground beef (80/20 blend)
  • ¼ lb ground pork
  • ¼ cup panko breadcrumbs
  • ¼ cup whole milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper
  • 1 tbsp avocado oil

Ingredients (Sauce)

  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour (or 1 tsp cornstarch for gluten-free)
  • 1 cup beef broth (low-sodium)
  • ½ cup sour cream (or Greek yogurt)
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce (or gluten-free version)
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Ingredients (Bowl Base)

  • 3 cups cooked cauliflower rice or egg noodles per serving
  • 2 tbsp sliced green onions
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill or parsley

Instructions (Meatballs)

  1. Soak breadcrumbs in milk for 2 minutes.
  2. Combine ground beef, ground pork, soaked breadcrumbs, egg, garlic, thyme, salt, pepper, nutmeg, and cayenne in a bowl. Mix gently until just combined. Do not overwork.
  3. Form into 16 meatballs (about 1 oz each).
  4. Heat 1 tbsp avocado oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Working in batches, cook meatballs 8–10 min, turning occasionally, until cooked through (160°F internal temp). Remove to a plate.

Instructions (Sauce)

  1. In the same skillet, melt 1 tbsp butter over medium heat. Sprinkle flour; whisk constantly for 1 minute to create a roux.
  2. Slowly add beef broth while whisking to prevent lumps. Bring to a simmer.
  3. Remove from heat. Whisk in sour cream, Dijon mustard, and Worcestershire sauce until smooth.
  4. Return meatballs to skillet. Simmer gently for 3–5 min to warm through and allow flavors to meld. Do not boil or sour cream may curdle.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Stir in fresh parsley.

Instructions (Assembly)

  1. Divide cooked cauliflower rice or noodles between two bowls.
  2. Top each with Swedish meatballs and creamy sauce.
  3. Garnish with sliced green onions and fresh dill. Serve immediately.
Protein: 42 g
Carbs: 16 g
Fat: 28 g
Fiber: 2 g

Meal Prep Notes

Form meatballs ahead and refrigerate up to 24 hours or freeze for 3 months. Cook fresh or reheat gently at 350°F for 10 min. Prepare sauce fresh for best consistency. Excellent comfort food for meal prep.
