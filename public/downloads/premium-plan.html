Macro Guide & Portion Cheatsheet

Your quick-reference guide to building balanced meals. Use these hand-based portions for easy measuring anywhere.

Portion Guide

✋
Protein Palm-sized portion per meal (approx. 4-6 oz). Chicken, fish, turkey, eggs.
😐
Carbohydrates Cupped-hand portion (approx. 1/2 to 1 cup). Rice, quinoa, oats, sweet potato.
👍
Fats Thumb-sized portion (approx. 1 tbsp). Olive oil, butter, nut butters, avocado.
✊
Vegetables Fist-sized or more per meal. Load up freely -- broccoli, greens, peppers, asparagus.

Meal Building Tips

Step 1: Start with Protein Every meal anchors around a palm-sized protein source. This keeps you full and supports muscle recovery.
Step 2: Add Smart Carbs Choose whole-food carbs like quinoa, rice, sweet potato, or oats. One cupped-hand per meal.
Step 3: Include Healthy Fats A thumb of healthy fat -- olive oil, avocado, nuts -- adds satiety and flavor.
Step 4: Fill with Vegetables Fill at least half your plate with non-starchy vegetables for fiber and micronutrients.

1. Garlic Butter Chicken Skillet

15 min 480 cal 36g protein

Tender chicken cutlets seasoned with paprika and thyme, seared golden, then finished in a rich garlic butter honey sauce. A weeknight staple that tastes like a restaurant dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (sliced into 4 cutlets)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. Slice chicken breasts in half horizontally to create 4 thin cutlets. Pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Season both sides generously with salt, paprika, thyme, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear chicken cutlets for 2-3 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  4. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter to the same skillet. Once melted, add minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Stir in honey and Italian seasoning. Return chicken to the skillet and spoon the garlic butter sauce over the top. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

2. Lemon Herb Chicken Bowl

25 min 520 cal 42g protein

A satisfying quinoa bowl with charred broccoli, lemon-herb chicken, and creamy avocado. Bright, filling, and perfect for meal prep.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast
  • 2 cups quinoa, cooked
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 lemon (juice + zest)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

Instructions

  1. Season chicken breast with garlic powder, oregano, salt, pepper, and half the lemon juice. Sear in olive oil over medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes per side until cooked through. Let rest, then slice.
  2. In the same pan, add broccoli florets and cook over high heat for 3-4 minutes until charred in spots but still crisp-tender.
  3. Build bowls: divide cooked quinoa among bowls, top with sliced chicken, charred broccoli, and avocado slices.
  4. Drizzle with remaining lemon juice, sprinkle with lemon zest and parsley. Serve immediately.

3. Turkey Taco Lettuce Cups

20 min 410 cal 38g protein

All the bold flavor of taco night with a fraction of the carbs. Seasoned ground turkey spooned into crisp romaine cups with fresh toppings.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 head romaine lettuce, separated into cups
  • 1/2 cup salsa
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt (as topping)

Instructions

  1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add ground turkey and cook, breaking it into crumbles, until fully browned (about 6-7 minutes).
  2. Add chili powder, cumin, and paprika. Stir well and cook for another minute to let the spices bloom.
  3. Stir in salsa and diced tomatoes. Simmer for 2-3 minutes until the mixture is heated through and slightly thickened.
  4. Spoon turkey mixture into romaine lettuce cups. Top with cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and a dollop of Greek yogurt.

5. Salmon & Avocado Plate

22 min 560 cal 40g protein

Crispy-skinned salmon alongside roasted asparagus, cool cucumber, and creamy avocado. A beautiful plate loaded with healthy fats and protein.

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 1/2 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 lemon (juice + wedges)
  • 1 tsp dried dill
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Season salmon fillets with garlic powder, dill, salt, and pepper. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and sear salmon skin-side down for 4 minutes, flip and cook 3 more minutes until golden and cooked through.
  2. Toss asparagus with a drizzle of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast at 425°F for 10-12 minutes until tender and lightly charred.
  3. Plate salmon alongside roasted asparagus, sliced avocado, and cucumber. Squeeze lemon over everything and serve with lemon wedges.

6. Chickpea Power Salad

15 min 460 cal 22g protein

A hearty meal-prep salad with massaged kale, protein-rich chickpeas, bright vegetables, and a creamy lemon-tahini dressing that gets better overnight.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups kale, stems removed and chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 lemon (juiced)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Place chopped kale in a large bowl. Add a pinch of salt and a squeeze of lemon juice. Massage with your hands for 1-2 minutes until the kale softens and deepens in color.
  2. Add chickpeas, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and crumbled feta to the kale. Toss gently to combine.
  3. Whisk together tahini, remaining lemon juice, minced garlic, a splash of water, salt, and pepper until smooth and pourable.
  4. Drizzle dressing over the salad and toss to coat. Serve immediately or refrigerate for meal prep.

7. Egg-White Breakfast Skillet

15 min 290 cal 32g protein

A big-volume, low-calorie breakfast skillet packed with colorful vegetables and fluffy egg whites. High protein, high satisfaction.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups egg whites
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 2 tbsp crumbled feta cheese
  • Cooking spray
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat and coat with cooking spray. Add diced bell pepper and sliced mushrooms. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add spinach and cook for 1-2 minutes until wilted.
  3. Pour egg whites over the vegetables. Cook, gently stirring, for 3-4 minutes until the egg whites are set and fluffy.
  4. Top with crumbled feta cheese, season with salt and pepper, and serve hot.

8. One-Pan Chicken & Sweet Potato

30 min 510 cal 38g protein

Everything on one sheet pan, everything in the oven, minimal cleanup. Roasted sweet potatoes, seasoned chicken, and tender broccoli -- dinner made easy.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 2 sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. Toss cubed sweet potatoes with half the olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread on the sheet pan and roast for 10 minutes.
  3. Season chicken breasts with Italian seasoning, remaining garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Add chicken and broccoli florets (tossed with remaining olive oil) to the sheet pan.
  4. Return to oven and bake for 20-25 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and sweet potatoes are tender. Slice chicken and serve everything together.

9. Turkey & Black Bean Bowl

20 min 490 cal 36g protein

A Tex-Mex-inspired bowl with seasoned ground turkey, warm black beans, fluffy rice, and all the fresh toppings. Big flavor, big protein.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup rice, cooked
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup salsa
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions

  1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook ground turkey, breaking into crumbles, until browned (6-7 minutes).
  2. Add cumin and chili powder. Stir and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.

  4. Build bowls: divide rice among bowls, top with seasoned turkey, warm black beans, sliced avocado, and salsa. Garnish with cilantro and a squeeze of lime.

10. Mediterranean Tuna Salad

10 min 380 cal 34g protein

A bright, no-cook lunch that comes together in minutes. Protein-packed tuna tossed with Mediterranean vegetables, olives, and a simple lemon-olive oil dressing.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (5 oz each) tuna, drained
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp kalamata olives, halved
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon (juiced)
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 4 cups mixed greens
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Drain tuna and flake into a large bowl with a fork.
  2. Add halved cherry tomatoes, diced cucumber, sliced red onion, and kalamata olives to the bowl.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice. Sprinkle with oregano, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to combine.
  4. Divide mixed greens among plates and top with the tuna salad. Serve immediately.

11. Chicken Stir-Fry

20 min 450 cal 35g protein

A quick weeknight stir-fry with tender chicken, crisp snap peas, and a savory-sweet tamari glaze. Better than takeout, ready in 20 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced thin
  • 2 cups snap peas
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 tbsp tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat a large skillet or wok over high heat with a drizzle of oil. Sear sliced chicken for 3-4 minutes until golden and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, stir-fry snap peas, bell pepper, and broccoli for 2-3 minutes until crisp-tender.
  3. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Whisk together tamari, honey, and sesame oil. Return chicken to the pan, pour in the sauce, and toss everything to coat. Serve over cooked rice.

12. Protein Overnight Oats

5 min prep 420 cal 30g protein

Prep tonight, grab-and-go tomorrow. Creamy overnight oats packed with protein from Greek yogurt and protein powder, topped with fresh berries.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1/2 cup berries (for topping)
  • 1 tsp honey

Instructions

  1. In a mason jar or container, combine rolled oats, Greek yogurt, milk, protein powder, and chia seeds. Stir until well mixed.
  2. Seal and refrigerate overnight (or at least 4 hours).
  3. In the morning, top with fresh berries and a drizzle of honey. Eat cold straight from the jar or warm in the microwave for 1-2 minutes.

13. Ground Turkey Stuffed Zucchini

25 min 380 cal 34g protein

Hollowed-out zucchini boats loaded with savory seasoned turkey, diced tomatoes, and melted mozzarella. A low-carb dinner that feels indulgent.

Ingredients

  • 4 zucchini, halved lengthwise
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil, for garnish
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Use a spoon to scoop out the center of each zucchini half, creating a shallow boat. Place cut-side up on a lined baking sheet.
  2. Brown ground turkey in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Cook for 1 minute.
  3. Stir in diced tomatoes and simmer for 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened. Spoon the turkey mixture into each zucchini boat.
  4. Top with shredded mozzarella. Bake for 15 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly and zucchini is tender. Garnish with fresh basil.

14. Asian Chicken Lettuce Wraps

15 min 350 cal 30g protein

Light but deeply satisfying lettuce wraps with savory ground chicken, crunchy water chestnuts, and a sweet-savory hoisin-ginger sauce. Addictively good.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground chicken
  • 1 head butter lettuce, leaves separated
  • 1 can (8 oz) water chestnuts, diced
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • Sriracha, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat with a drizzle of oil. Add ground chicken and cook, breaking into small crumbles, until browned (5-6 minutes).
  2. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Stir in hoisin sauce, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and diced water chestnuts. Cook for 2 minutes, tossing to combine and heat through.
  4. Spoon the chicken mixture into butter lettuce cups. Top with sliced green onions and a drizzle of sriracha. Serve immediately.

15. Sheet-Pan Honey Garlic Shrimp

20 min 380 cal 32g protein

Succulent shrimp tossed in a sticky honey-garlic glaze and roasted alongside crisp snap peas and bell peppers. One pan, maximum flavor, minimal effort.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups snap peas
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. Whisk together honey, soy sauce, and minced garlic in a small bowl.
  3. Toss shrimp, snap peas, and bell pepper with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on the sheet pan in a single layer.
  4. Pour the honey-garlic sauce over the shrimp and vegetables. Roast for 8-10 minutes until shrimp are pink and curled.
  5. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve immediately over rice or as-is.

16. Beef & Broccoli Stir-Fry

20 min 480 cal 38g protein

The takeout classic made at home in 20 minutes. Tender strips of flank steak in a rich, savory sauce with crisp-tender broccoli over steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb flank steak, sliced thin against the grain
  • 4 cups broccoli florets
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch (+ 2 tbsp water)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce, brown sugar, cornstarch slurry, and sesame oil in a small bowl. Set aside.
  2. Heat a large skillet or wok over high heat with a drizzle of oil. Sear flank steak strips for 2-3 minutes until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, stir-fry broccoli florets for 3-4 minutes until bright green and crisp-tender. Add garlic and ginger, cook 30 seconds.
  4. Return beef to the pan, pour in the sauce, and toss everything together until the sauce thickens and coats evenly (about 1 minute). Serve over rice.

17. Cottage-Cheese Pancakes

15 min 340 cal 28g protein

Fluffy, high-protein pancakes that taste indulgent but fuel your goals. The cottage cheese melts into the batter for extra creaminess and protein.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cottage cheese
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • Fresh berries, for topping
  • Maple syrup, for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Blend cottage cheese, eggs, oats, vanilla, cinnamon, and baking powder in a blender until smooth.
  2. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat and coat lightly with cooking spray or butter.
  3. Pour 1/4 cup batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface (about 2 minutes), then flip and cook 1-2 minutes more until golden.
  4. Stack pancakes and top with fresh berries and a light drizzle of maple syrup.

20. Pesto Zoodle Pasta

15 min 350 cal 28g protein

Low-carb zucchini noodles tossed in vibrant basil pesto with seared chicken, burst cherry tomatoes, and a shower of parmesan. Light yet satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 4 zucchini, spiralized
  • 2 chicken breasts, sliced
  • 3 tbsp basil pesto
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan
  • 1 tbsp pine nuts
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear sliced chicken for 4-5 minutes until golden and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add cherry tomatoes and cook for 2 minutes until they begin to burst.

21. Black Bean & Sweet Potato Tacos

25 min 420 cal 18g protein

Hearty plant-forward tacos with roasted sweet potato, seasoned black beans, and a cool lime crema. Proof that vegetarian meals can be deeply satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 2 sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 lime (juice + wedges)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss cubed sweet potatoes with olive oil, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  2. Warm black beans in a small saucepan with a pinch of cumin. Warm corn tortillas in a dry skillet or directly over a gas flame.
  3. Make lime crema: mix Greek yogurt with half the lime juice and a pinch of salt until smooth.
  4. Assemble tacos: fill each tortilla with roasted sweet potato, black beans, avocado slices, a drizzle of lime crema, and fresh cilantro. Serve with lime wedges.

22. Buffalo Chicken Stuffed Peppers

30 min 390 cal 34g protein

All the flavor of buffalo wings packed into colorful bell peppers. Shredded chicken in buffalo-ranch sauce, baked until bubbly and irresistible.

Ingredients

  • 4 bell peppers, tops cut off and seeded
  • 2 cups shredded chicken (rotisserie works great)
  • 1/3 cup buffalo sauce
  • 1/4 cup ranch dressing

23. Turkey Meatball Marinara

25 min 420 cal 36g protein

Juicy, herb-seasoned turkey meatballs simmered in rich marinara sauce. Served over spaghetti squash for a low-carb twist on an Italian classic.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • Fresh basil, for garnish
  • Spaghetti squash, for serving
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground turkey, egg, breadcrumbs, parmesan, garlic, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Mix until just combined. Roll into 12-14 meatballs.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat with a drizzle of olive oil. Brown meatballs on all sides (about 4 minutes total). They don't need to be cooked through yet.
  3. Pour marinara sauce into the skillet. Nestle the meatballs in the sauce, cover, and simmer for 12-15 minutes until meatballs are cooked through.
  4. Serve meatballs and sauce over roasted spaghetti squash strands. Garnish with fresh basil and extra parmesan.

25. Cajun Cod with Green Beans

20 min 340 cal 32g protein

Flaky, cajun-spiced cod fillets with a squeeze of lemon alongside garlicky sautéed green beans. A lean, flavorful dinner that comes together fast.

Ingredients

  • 2 cod fillets (6 oz each)
  • 2 tsp cajun seasoning
  • 3 cups green beans, trimmed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon (juice + wedges)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Pat cod fillets dry and season both sides with cajun seasoning, salt, and pepper.

  4. Squeeze lemon juice over the green beans. Plate cod alongside green beans and serve with lemon wedges.

27. Shrimp & Asparagus Skillet

15 min 320 cal 30g protein

A quick, elegant skillet dinner. Plump shrimp and tender asparagus in a bright lemon-butter sauce with a kick of red pepper flakes.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon (juice + zest)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tbsp butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add asparagus and cook for 3-4 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp. Remove and set aside.
  2. Add remaining butter to the skillet. Sear shrimp for 1-2 minutes per side until pink and curled.
  3. Add minced garlic and red pepper flakes, cook 30 seconds. Squeeze in lemon juice and add lemon zest.
  4. Return asparagus to the skillet, toss to combine, and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve immediately.

28. BBQ Turkey Burgers

20 min 380 cal 32g protein

Juicy turkey burgers brushed with tangy BBQ sauce. Served in lettuce wraps for a lighter take or on whole wheat buns when you want the full experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 3 tbsp BBQ sauce (+ extra for serving)
  • 1/4 onion, finely diced
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Lettuce wraps or whole wheat buns
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • Pickles, sliced
  • Red onion, sliced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine ground turkey, 2 tbsp BBQ sauce, diced onion, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix gently and form into 4 patties.
  2. Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook patties for 4-5 minutes per side until cooked through (internal temp 165°F).
  3. During the last minute of cooking, brush tops with remaining BBQ sauce.
  4. Serve in lettuce wraps or on buns with sliced tomato, pickles, and red onion.

29. Greek Power Bowl

15 min 450 cal 30g protein

A Mediterranean-inspired bowl with fluffy quinoa, grilled chicken, crisp vegetables, briny olives, and tangy feta drizzled with lemon-herb vinaigrette.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 2 grilled chicken breasts, sliced
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tbsp kalamata olives, halved
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • Lemon-herb vinaigrette (olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, garlic)

Instructions

  1. Divide cooked quinoa among bowls as the base.
  2. Arrange sliced grilled chicken, diced cucumber, cherry tomatoes, kalamata olives, red onion, and crumbled feta on top of the quinoa.
  3. Whisk together 2 tbsp olive oil, juice of 1 lemon, 1/2 tsp oregano, and 1 minced garlic clove for the vinaigrette.
  4. Drizzle vinaigrette generously over the bowl and serve.

30. Coconut Lime Chicken

25 min 460 cal 35g protein

Tender chicken simmered in a fragrant coconut-lime sauce with hints of garlic and ginger. Served over rice, this is comfort food with a tropical twist.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts, sliced
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) light coconut milk
  • 2 limes (juice + zest)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Cooked rice, for serving
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Season chicken slices with salt and pepper. Sear in a hot skillet with a drizzle of oil for 3-4 minutes per side until golden. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, sauté garlic and ginger for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Pour in coconut milk, lime juice, lime zest, and red pepper flakes. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 5 minutes until slightly thickened.
  4. Return chicken to the sauce and simmer for 3-4 minutes until cooked through. Garnish with cilantro and serve over rice.

31. Egg & Veggie Breakfast Muffins

25 min 180 cal each (3 = 540 cal) 24g protein per 3

Make a batch, eat all week. Portable egg muffins loaded with spinach, peppers, and feta -- grab-and-go breakfasts that actually taste great reheated.

Ingredients

  • 8 eggs
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, finely diced
  • 1/4 onion, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Cooking spray
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Spray a 12-cup muffin tin generously with cooking spray.
  2. Whisk eggs in a large bowl with salt and pepper. Stir in chopped spinach, diced bell pepper, diced onion, and crumbled feta.
  3. Pour the egg mixture evenly into the 12 muffin cups (about 3/4 full each).
  4. Bake for 18-20 minutes until the eggs are set and tops are lightly golden. Let cool for 2 minutes before removing. Store in the fridge for up to 5 days.

32. Teriyaki Salmon Bowl

20 min 520 cal 38g protein

Glazed teriyaki salmon over warm rice with edamame, cool cucumber, creamy avocado, and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. A restaurant-quality bowl at home.

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cornstarch (+ 1 tbsp water)
  • 1.5 cups cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup edamame, shelled
  • 1/2 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Make teriyaki sauce: whisk soy sauce, honey, ginger, garlic, and cornstarch slurry in a small bowl.
  2. Sear salmon fillets skin-side down in a hot skillet for 4 minutes. Flip and cook 3 more minutes.
  3. Pour teriyaki sauce over the salmon in the skillet. Cook for 1-2 minutes, spooning sauce over the fish until it thickens and glazes.
  4. Build bowls: rice on the bottom, topped with teriyaki salmon, edamame, cucumber, and avocado. Drizzle remaining sauce over the top and sprinkle with sesame seeds.

33. Loaded Sweet Potato

15 min 440 cal 28g protein

A microwave-ready sweet potato loaded with protein-packed toppings: black beans, grilled chicken, melted cheese, and a cool yogurt-salsa finish.

Ingredients

  • 2 large sweet potatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup grilled chicken, diced
  • 2 tbsp Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup salsa
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Pierce sweet potatoes with a fork several times. Microwave on high for 8-10 minutes (turning halfway) until soft, or bake at 400°F for 45 minutes.
  2. Split sweet potatoes open and fluff the flesh with a fork.
  3. Top each potato with warm black beans, diced grilled chicken, and shredded cheese. Microwave for 30 seconds to melt the cheese.
  4. Finish with a dollop of Greek yogurt, salsa, and sliced green onions.

34. Spaghetti Squash Bolognese

35 min 380 cal 30g protein

A low-carb twist on the classic. Rich, meaty Bolognese sauce made with lean ground beef ladled over tender spaghetti squash strands. Comfort food, lightened up.

Ingredients

  • 1 spaghetti squash, halved and seeded
  • 1 lb lean ground beef
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan
  • Fresh basil, for garnish
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Place spaghetti squash halves cut-side down on a lined baking sheet. Roast for 25-30 minutes until tender. Scrape flesh with a fork to create strands.
  2. While squash roasts, brown ground beef in a skillet over medium-high heat. Drain any excess fat. Add diced onion and cook for 3 minutes.
  3. Add minced garlic and Italian seasoning, cook 30 seconds. Pour in crushed tomatoes, season with salt and pepper. Simmer for 10-12 minutes until thickened.
  4. Divide spaghetti squash strands among plates, top with Bolognese sauce, grated parmesan, and fresh basil.

35. Five Smart Snacks Under 200 Cal Each

Bonus Snack Pack 90-190 cal each

Five satisfying snack recipes to keep between meals interesting. Each clocks in under 200 calories -- perfect for curbing cravings without derailing your progress.

A. Coconut Energy Balls

120 cal per serving

Ingredients: 1/2 cup oats, 2 tbsp peanut butter, 1 tbsp honey, 2 tbsp shredded coconut, 1 tbsp dark chocolate chips.

Instructions: Mix all ingredients in a bowl until combined. Roll into 6-8 small balls. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to set. Store in the fridge for up to 5 days.

B. Almond-Butter Apple Slices

190 cal per serving

Ingredients: 1 apple (sliced), 1 tbsp almond butter, pinch of cinnamon.

Instructions: Slice apple and arrange on a plate. Drizzle or dip in almond butter. Sprinkle with cinnamon. Enjoy immediately.

C. Dark-Chocolate Trail Mix

180 cal per serving

Ingredients: 1 tbsp dark chocolate chips, 10 almonds, 1 tbsp dried cranberries, 1 tbsp pumpkin seeds.

Instructions: Combine all ingredients in a small container or bag. Portion into single servings for grab-and-go snacking throughout the week.

D. Cucumber Cream Cheese Bites

90 cal per serving

Ingredients: 1 cucumber (sliced into rounds), 2 tbsp cream cheese, everything bagel seasoning.

Instructions: Spread a small amount of cream cheese on each cucumber round. Sprinkle with everything bagel seasoning. Serve immediately or refrigerate.

E. Banana Protein Bites

150 cal per serving

Ingredients: 1 ripe banana (mashed), 1 scoop protein powder, 1/4 cup oats, 1 tbsp peanut butter, 1 tsp honey.

Instructions: Mash banana in a bowl. Mix in protein powder, oats, peanut butter, and honey until a thick dough forms. Roll into 8 small bites. Refrigerate for 20 minutes. Store in the fridge for up to 4 days.
