1. Garlic Butter Chicken Skillet

15 min 4 servings

Tender chicken cutlets seasoned with paprika and thyme, seared golden, then finished in a rich garlic butter honey sauce. A weeknight staple that tastes like a restaurant dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (sliced into 4 cutlets)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. Slice chicken breasts in half horizontally to create 4 thin cutlets. Pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Season both sides generously with salt, paprika, thyme, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear chicken cutlets for 2-3 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  4. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter to the same skillet. Once melted, add minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Stir in honey and Italian seasoning. Return chicken to the skillet and spoon the garlic butter sauce over the top. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

2. Lemon Herb Chicken Bowl

25 min 42g protein

A satisfying quinoa bowl with charred broccoli, lemon-herb chicken, and creamy avocado. Bright, filling, and perfect for meal prep.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast
  • 2 cups quinoa, cooked
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 lemon (juice + zest)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

Instructions

  1. Season chicken breast with garlic powder, oregano, salt, pepper, and half the lemon juice. Sear in olive oil over medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes per side until cooked through. Let rest, then slice.
  2. In the same pan, add broccoli florets and cook over high heat for 3-4 minutes until charred in spots but still crisp-tender.
  3. Build bowls: divide cooked quinoa among bowls, top with sliced chicken, charred broccoli, and avocado slices.
  4. Drizzle with remaining lemon juice, sprinkle with lemon zest and parsley. Serve immediately.

3. Turkey Taco Lettuce Cups

20 min 38g protein

All the bold flavor of taco night with a fraction of the carbs. Seasoned ground turkey spooned into crisp romaine cups with fresh toppings.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 head romaine lettuce, separated into cups
  • 1/2 cup salsa
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt (as topping)

Instructions

  1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add ground turkey and cook, breaking it into crumbles, until fully browned (about 6-7 minutes).
  2. Add chili powder, cumin, and paprika. Stir well and cook for another minute to let the spices bloom.
  3. Stir in salsa and diced tomatoes. Simmer for 2-3 minutes until the mixture is heated through and slightly thickened.
  4. Spoon turkey mixture into romaine lettuce cups. Top with cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and a dollop of Greek yogurt.

4. Greek Yogurt Berry Bowl

5 min 28g protein

The fastest high-protein breakfast. Thick Greek yogurt layered with fresh berries, crunchy granola, and a drizzle of honey. Simple, satisfying, done.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup mixed berries (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tbsp granola
  • 1 tsp chia seeds
  • 1 tsp honey

Instructions

  1. Add Greek yogurt to a bowl.
  2. Top with mixed berries, granola, and chia seeds.
  3. Drizzle with honey and serve immediately.

5. Salmon & Avocado Plate

22 min 40g protein

Crispy-skinned salmon alongside roasted asparagus, cool cucumber, and creamy avocado. A beautiful plate loaded with healthy fats and protein.

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 1/2 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 lemon (juice + wedges)
  • 1 tsp dried dill
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Season salmon fillets with garlic powder, dill, salt, and pepper. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and sear salmon skin-side down for 4 minutes, flip and cook 3 more minutes until golden and cooked through.
  2. Toss asparagus with a drizzle of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast at 425°F for 10-12 minutes until tender and lightly charred.
  3. Plate salmon alongside roasted asparagus, sliced avocado, and cucumber. Squeeze lemon over everything and serve with lemon wedges.

6. Chickpea Power Salad

15 min 22g protein

A hearty meal-prep salad with massaged kale, protein-rich chickpeas, bright vegetables, and a creamy lemon-tahini dressing that gets better overnight.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups kale, stems removed and chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 lemon (juiced)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Place chopped kale in a large bowl. Add a pinch of salt and a squeeze of lemon juice. Massage with your hands for 1-2 minutes until the kale softens and deepens in color.
  2. Add chickpeas, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and crumbled feta to the kale. Toss gently to combine.
  3. Whisk together tahini, remaining lemon juice, minced garlic, a splash of water, salt, and pepper until smooth and pourable.
  4. Drizzle dressing over the salad and toss to coat. Serve immediately or refrigerate for meal prep.

7. Egg-White Breakfast Skillet

15 min 32g protein

A big-volume, low-calorie breakfast skillet packed with colorful vegetables and fluffy egg whites. High protein, high satisfaction.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups egg whites
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 2 tbsp crumbled feta cheese
  • Cooking spray
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat and coat with cooking spray. Add diced bell pepper and sliced mushrooms. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add spinach and cook for 1-2 minutes until wilted.
  3. Pour egg whites over the vegetables. Cook, gently stirring, for 3-4 minutes until the egg whites are set and fluffy.
  4. Top with crumbled feta cheese, season with salt and pepper, and serve hot.

8. One-Pan Chicken & Sweet Potato

30 min 38g protein

Everything on one sheet pan, everything in the oven, minimal cleanup. Roasted sweet potatoes, seasoned chicken, and tender broccoli -- dinner made easy.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 2 sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. Toss cubed sweet potatoes with half the olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread on the sheet pan and roast for 10 minutes.
  3. Season chicken breasts with Italian seasoning, remaining garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Add chicken and broccoli florets (tossed with remaining olive oil) to the sheet pan.
  4. Return to oven and bake for 20-25 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and sweet potatoes are tender. Slice chicken and serve everything together.

9. Turkey & Black Bean Bowl

20 min 36g protein

A Tex-Mex-inspired bowl with seasoned ground turkey, warm black beans, fluffy rice, and all the fresh toppings. Big flavor, big protein.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup rice, cooked
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup salsa
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions

  1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook ground turkey, breaking into crumbles, until browned (6-7 minutes).
  2. Add cumin and chili powder. Stir and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Warm black beans in a small saucepan or in the microwave.
  4. Build bowls: divide rice among bowls, top with seasoned turkey, warm black beans, sliced avocado, and salsa. Garnish with cilantro and a squeeze of lime.

10. Mediterranean Tuna Salad

10 min 34g protein

A bright, no-cook lunch that comes together in minutes. Protein-packed tuna tossed with Mediterranean vegetables, olives, and a simple lemon-olive oil dressing.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (5 oz each) tuna, drained
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp kalamata olives, halved
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon (juiced)
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 4 cups mixed greens
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Drain tuna and flake into a large bowl with a fork.
  2. Add halved cherry tomatoes, diced cucumber, sliced red onion, and kalamata olives to the bowl.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice. Sprinkle with oregano, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to combine.
  4. Divide mixed greens among plates and top with the tuna salad. Serve immediately.

11. Chicken Stir-Fry with Vegetables

20 min 35g protein

A quick weeknight stir-fry with tender chicken, crisp snap peas, and a savory-sweet tamari glaze. Better than takeout, ready in 20 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced thin
  • 2 cups snap peas
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 tbsp tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat a large skillet or wok over high heat with a drizzle of oil. Sear sliced chicken for 3-4 minutes until golden and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, stir-fry snap peas, bell pepper, and broccoli for 2-3 minutes until crisp-tender.
  3. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Whisk together tamari, honey, and sesame oil. Return chicken to the pan, pour in the sauce, and toss everything to coat. Serve over cooked rice.

12. Protein Overnight Oats

5 min prep 30g protein

Prep tonight, grab-and-go tomorrow. Creamy overnight oats packed with protein from Greek yogurt and protein powder, topped with fresh berries.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1/2 cup berries (for topping)
  • 1 tsp honey

Instructions

  1. In a mason jar or container, combine rolled oats, Greek yogurt, milk, protein powder, and chia seeds. Stir until well mixed.
  2. Seal and refrigerate overnight (or at least 4 hours).
  3. In the morning, top with fresh berries and a drizzle of honey. Eat cold straight from the jar or warm in the microwave for 1-2 minutes.

13. Ground Turkey Stuffed Zucchini

25 min 34g protein

Hollowed-out zucchini boats loaded with savory seasoned turkey, diced tomatoes, and melted mozzarella. A low-carb dinner that feels indulgent.

Ingredients

  • 4 zucchini, halved lengthwise
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil, for garnish
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Use a spoon to scoop out the center of each zucchini half, creating a shallow boat. Place cut-side up on a lined baking sheet.
  2. Brown ground turkey in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Cook for 1 minute.
  3. Stir in diced tomatoes and simmer for 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened. Spoon the turkey mixture into each zucchini boat.
  4. Top with shredded mozzarella. Bake for 15 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly and zucchini is tender. Garnish with fresh basil.

14. Asian Chicken Lettuce Wraps

15 min 30g protein

Light but deeply satisfying lettuce wraps with savory ground chicken, crunchy water chestnuts, and a sweet-savory hoisin-ginger sauce. Addictively good.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground chicken
  • 1 head butter lettuce, leaves separated
  • 1 can (8 oz) water chestnuts, diced
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • Sriracha, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat with a drizzle of oil. Add ground chicken and cook, breaking into small crumbles, until browned (5-6 minutes).
  2. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Stir in hoisin sauce, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and diced water chestnuts. Cook for 2 minutes, tossing to combine and heat through.
  4. Spoon the chicken mixture into butter lettuce cups. Top with sliced green onions and a drizzle of sriracha. Serve immediately.
