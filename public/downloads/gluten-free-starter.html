1. Herb-Crusted Salmon & Sweet Potato

GF
25 min 540 cal 38g protein

Oven-baked salmon with a golden Dijon-herb crust, roasted sweet potato rounds, and a bed of wilted spinach. A nutrient-dense dinner that comes together on one sheet pan.

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, sliced into rounds
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard (GF-certified)
  • Fresh dill, chopped
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Garlic powder

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Slice sweet potatoes into 1/4-inch rounds, toss with olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Spread on a sheet pan and roast for 20 minutes.
  2. While potatoes roast, brush salmon fillets with Dijon mustard and press chopped dill and parsley onto the top to form an herb crust. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. After 20 minutes, push sweet potatoes to one side. Place salmon on the sheet pan and return to oven for 12 minutes until salmon flakes easily with a fork.
  4. Heat a small pan over medium heat. Add spinach and wilt for 1-2 minutes, seasoning with a pinch of salt. Plate salmon over spinach alongside sweet potato rounds.

2. Grilled Chicken with Mango Salsa

GF
20 min 480 cal 36g protein

Juicy grilled chicken topped with a bright, fresh mango-jalapeno salsa, served over fluffy cilantro-lime rice. Naturally gluten-free and bursting with tropical flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1/2 jalapeno, seeded and minced
  • 2 tbsp red onion, finely diced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 limes
  • 1 cup white rice
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions

  1. Cook rice according to package directions. When done, fluff with a fork and stir in the juice of 1 lime and a handful of chopped cilantro. Cover and set aside.
  2. Season chicken breasts with cumin, salt, and a drizzle of olive oil. Grill or pan-sear over medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes per side until cooked through (internal temp 165°F). Let rest 5 minutes, then slice.
  3. While chicken rests, make the salsa: combine diced mango, minced jalapeno, red onion, cilantro, and the juice of 1 lime. Toss gently and season with a pinch of salt.
  4. Serve sliced chicken over cilantro-lime rice, topped generously with mango salsa.

3. Turkey & Zucchini Meatballs

GF
25 min 390 cal 34g protein

Tender baked meatballs made with ground turkey and shredded zucchini -- no breadcrumbs needed. Almond flour keeps them perfectly bound and 100% gluten-free.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 zucchini, shredded and squeezed dry
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp almond flour
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • GF marinara sauce, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Shred the zucchini using a box grater, then squeeze out as much moisture as possible using a clean towel or cheesecloth.
  3. In a large bowl, combine ground turkey, squeezed zucchini, egg, almond flour, minced garlic, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Mix until just combined -- don't overmix.
  4. Roll the mixture into 16 even meatballs (about 1.5 inches each). Place on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 18-20 minutes until golden and cooked through. Serve with warm GF marinara sauce.

4. Shrimp Cauliflower Fried Rice

GF
15 min 350 cal 32g protein

A low-carb stir-fry that tastes like the real thing. Cauliflower rice, plump shrimp, scrambled eggs, and crisp veggies tossed in tamari -- naturally gluten-free and ready in 15 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cups riced cauliflower
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup peas and carrots (fresh or frozen)
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tbsp tamari (GF soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

Instructions

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add shrimp and cook 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, scramble the eggs, breaking them into small pieces. Push to one side of the pan.
  3. Add riced cauliflower, peas and carrots, and minced garlic. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until cauliflower is tender but not mushy.
  4. Return shrimp to the pan. Pour tamari over everything and toss to combine. Top with sliced green onions and serve immediately.

5. Lemon Garlic Chicken & Green Beans

GF
20 min 420 cal 35g protein

One-pan chicken thighs with crispy golden skin, tender green beans, and a bright lemon-garlic finish. Simple, satisfying, and naturally gluten-free.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in chicken thighs
  • 3 cups green beans, trimmed
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1 lemon (juice + slices)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Pat chicken thighs dry and season generously with salt, pepper, and oregano on both sides.
  2. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Place chicken skin-side down and sear for 6 minutes without moving, until the skin is deeply golden and crispy.
  3. Flip the thighs. Add green beans, smashed garlic cloves, lemon slices, and red pepper flakes around the chicken.
  4. Transfer the skillet to the oven and cook for 12 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and green beans are tender. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over everything before serving.

6. Beef & Bell Pepper Skillet

GF
15 min 460 cal 36g protein

Quick-seared beef strips with colorful bell peppers in a savory tamari-ginger sauce. Served over steamed rice for a satisfying weeknight dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb flank steak, sliced thin against the grain
  • 3 bell peppers (mixed colors), sliced
  • 2 tbsp tamari (GF soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over high heat until smoking. Add sliced steak in a single layer and sear for 2-3 minutes until browned. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add sliced bell peppers. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until slightly softened but still crisp.
  3. Whisk together tamari, rice vinegar, grated ginger, and minced garlic in a small bowl.
  4. Return beef to the skillet, pour the sauce over everything, and toss to combine. Cook 1 minute more until heated through. Serve immediately over steamed rice.

7. Baked Cod with Tomato & Olive Relish

GF
25 min 320 cal 30g protein

Flaky Mediterranean baked cod topped with a vibrant cherry tomato, olive, and caper relish. Light, bright, and effortlessly elegant.

Ingredients

  • 2 cod fillets (6 oz each)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup kalamata olives, halved
  • 1 tbsp capers, drained
  • Fresh basil, torn
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon (juice + wedges)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Place cod fillets in a lightly oiled baking dish. Season with salt, pepper, minced garlic, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  2. In a small bowl, combine halved cherry tomatoes, kalamata olives, capers, a drizzle of olive oil, and a squeeze of lemon. Toss gently.
  3. Spoon the tomato-olive relish over and around the cod fillets.
  4. Bake for 15-18 minutes until cod is opaque and flakes easily with a fork. Garnish with torn fresh basil and serve with lemon wedges.

8. Chicken Avocado Rice Bowl

GF
20 min 490 cal 36g protein

Seasoned chicken with creamy avocado, warm black beans, and cilantro-lime rice. A filling, naturally gluten-free bowl that is endlessly customizable.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup white rice
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 limes
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Hot sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook rice according to package directions. When done, fluff and stir in the juice of 1 lime and a handful of chopped cilantro.
  2. Season chicken breasts with cumin, salt, and pepper. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat and cook chicken for 5-6 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Let rest, then slice.
  3. Warm black beans in a small saucepan or microwave. Season with a pinch of cumin and salt.
  4. Build bowls: divide cilantro-lime rice among bowls, top with sliced chicken, black beans, and avocado. Squeeze remaining lime over everything and add hot sauce to taste.

9. Stuffed Bell Peppers with Ground Turkey

GF
30 min 420 cal 32g protein

Colorful bell peppers stuffed with seasoned ground turkey, rice, diced tomatoes, and melted cheese. A hearty, family-friendly dinner that is naturally gluten-free.

Ingredients

  • 4 bell peppers, tops cut off and seeded
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Place hollowed bell peppers upright in a baking dish.
  2. Brown ground turkey in a skillet over medium-high heat, breaking into crumbles. Add minced garlic, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in cooked rice and diced tomatoes (with juices). Simmer for 2-3 minutes until combined. Spoon the filling into each bell pepper, packing gently.
  4. Top each pepper with shredded cheese. Cover baking dish with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove foil for the last 5 minutes to let cheese brown and bubble.

10. Sesame Ginger Salmon Bowl

GF
20 min 530 cal 38g protein

Pan-seared salmon glazed with a sweet and savory sesame-ginger sauce, served in a bowl with rice, edamame, and cool cucumber. An Asian-inspired favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 1 cup edamame, shelled
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 2 tbsp tamari (GF soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • Cooked rice, for serving
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish
  • 1 green onion, sliced

Instructions

  1. Whisk together tamari, honey, sesame oil, and grated ginger in a small bowl to make the glaze.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Season salmon with salt and pepper, then sear skin-side down for 4 minutes. Flip and cook 3 more minutes.
  3. Pour the glaze over the salmon in the last minute of cooking, spooning it over the fillets to coat. Remove from heat.
  4. Build bowls: divide cooked rice among bowls, add edamame, sliced cucumber, and glazed salmon. Drizzle any remaining sauce from the pan over the top. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onion.

11. Greek Chicken & Quinoa Salad

GF
15 min 470 cal 36g protein

A vibrant Mediterranean salad bowl with grilled chicken, fluffy quinoa, crisp vegetables, briny olives, and a bright lemon-oregano dressing.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup kalamata olives, halved
  • 2 tbsp red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Season chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and half the oregano. Grill or pan-sear over medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes per side until cooked through. Let rest, then slice.
  2. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, diced cucumber, halved cherry tomatoes, kalamata olives, and sliced red onion. Toss gently.
  3. Whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, remaining oregano, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  4. Divide the quinoa salad among bowls, top with sliced chicken and crumbled feta. Drizzle with lemon-oregano dressing and serve.

12. Pork Tenderloin & Roasted Vegetables

GF
30 min 440 cal 34g protein

Herb-rubbed pork tenderloin roasted alongside Brussels sprouts, carrots, and red onion on one sheet pan. Hearty, hands-off, and completely gluten-free.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork tenderloin
  • 2 cups Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 3 carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Pat pork tenderloin dry. Rub with 1 tbsp olive oil, rosemary, thyme, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Toss Brussels sprouts, carrots, and red onion with remaining olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a sheet pan in a single layer.
  3. Place the pork tenderloin in the center of the vegetables. Roast for 22-25 minutes until pork reaches 145°F and vegetables are golden and tender.
  4. Remove from oven and let the pork rest for 5 minutes before slicing into medallions. Serve alongside the roasted vegetables.

13. Coconut Curry Chicken

GF
25 min 480 cal 35g protein

Creamy coconut curry loaded with tender chicken, spinach, and bell pepper. Rich, warming, and naturally gluten-free -- make sure your curry paste is GF-certified.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast, cubed
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tbsp GF curry paste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • Cooked rice, for serving
  • Fresh cilantro and lime, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add cubed chicken and sear for 3-4 minutes until golden on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add minced garlic and grated ginger. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add GF curry paste and stir for 1 minute to bloom the spices.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and stir to combine. Return chicken to the pan along with sliced bell pepper. Simmer for 8-10 minutes until chicken is cooked through and sauce has thickened slightly.
  4. Stir in spinach and cook for 1-2 minutes until wilted. Serve over steamed rice, garnished with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime.

14. Egg & Veggie Breakfast Hash

GF
20 min 380 cal 24g protein

A hearty morning skillet with crispy sweet potato, colorful peppers and onion, topped with perfectly baked eggs. No bread needed -- this breakfast is all about the hash.

Ingredients

  • 2 sweet potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh chives, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add diced sweet potatoes and cook for 8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden and nearly tender.
  2. Add diced bell pepper and onion. Cook for 3 more minutes until softened. Season with smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Make 4 small wells in the hash. Crack an egg into each well. Cover the skillet with a lid (or foil) and cook for 4-5 minutes until egg whites are set but yolks are still slightly runny.
  4. Remove from heat, garnish with fresh chives, and serve directly from the skillet.

Gluten-Free Ingredient Guide

Essential swaps, label-reading tips, and cross-contamination awareness for confident GF cooking.

1 Common Gluten-Free Swaps

Instead Of Use This (GF) Notes
Soy sauce Tamari (GF-labeled) Traditional soy sauce contains wheat. Tamari is naturally brewed without wheat -- always check the label says "gluten-free."
Breadcrumbs Almond flour or GF breadcrumbs Almond flour adds protein and healthy fats. Works perfectly as a binder in meatballs, coatings, and crusts.
Pasta (wheat) Rice, quinoa, or GF pasta Rice and quinoa are naturally GF whole grains. If using GF pasta, look for rice-based or chickpea-based varieties.
Flour (all-purpose) Almond flour, rice flour, or GF blend For thickening sauces, use cornstarch or arrowroot. For baking, a 1:1 GF flour blend works best.
Regular mustard GF-certified Dijon mustard Some mustards contain wheat flour or malt vinegar. Look for "Certified Gluten-Free" on the label.
Hoisin / teriyaki sauce GF tamari + honey glaze Most commercial hoisin and teriyaki sauces contain wheat. A simple tamari-honey-ginger combo is a safe and delicious alternative.

2 Label Reading Tips

  • Look for "Certified Gluten-Free" -- this means the product has been tested to contain less than 20 ppm of gluten, the FDA threshold.
  • Read the allergen statement -- FDA requires wheat to be declared. However, barley and rye may not always be listed separately.
  • Watch for hidden gluten -- malt extract, malt vinegar, brewer's yeast, modified food starch (unless specified as corn), and hydrolyzed wheat protein all contain gluten.
  • Check spice blends and seasonings -- some contain wheat flour as an anti-caking agent. Buy pure, single-ingredient spices when possible.
  • "Wheat-free" does not mean "gluten-free" -- products may still contain barley or rye. Always look for the specific GF certification.

3 Cross-Contamination Notes

  • Dedicated cutting boards and utensils -- use separate tools for GF cooking to prevent trace contamination from wheat residue.
  • Clean surfaces thoroughly -- wipe down countertops, cutting boards, and cookware before preparing GF meals, especially in shared kitchens.
  • Separate cooking oils and water -- never reuse oil or water that has been used to fry or boil gluten-containing foods.
  • Toasters and colanders -- these are common sources of cross-contamination. Use dedicated GF-only versions or clean them meticulously.
  • Bulk bins at the grocery store -- scoops and bins may be shared between GF and gluten-containing products. Buy pre-packaged GF items instead.
  • Dining out -- always communicate your needs clearly. Ask about shared fryers, cooking surfaces, and ingredient sourcing.
